This is the final practice: three complete readings and a synthesis quiz. It teaches no new material. Every figure, rule, technique, and reporting form has already been introduced.

The work now is practice rather than instruction. You will read three unfamiliar charts. The first tests whether the method works outside the examples. The second tests whether you can hold the reading order when the chart is divided. The third asks one question in both systems and tests the discipline of keeping the two methods separate. Each reading has a **staged model solution** in three parts: cast verification, findings to weigh, and the model reading. Open them in order. Each final reading must use the six parts from Lesson 10: **answer, manner, end, timing, advice, limits**.

## The check-in

Before casting anything, have two things ready: the **Gerard card** from Lesson 12 and the **question map** from Lesson 11. If either is missing, finish it before beginning.

Retake Lesson 10's Exercise 4 before the first sitting and read your old answer critically: note what still holds, what now seems overstated, and what applies only to the shield method. Reading II tests the reading order directly.

## How to do the final practice

Do three sittings, one reading each, with at least a day between them. Do not run the three charts in one evening. Each sitting runs the worksheet in full, everything written down, no skipped steps.

**What is open.** Use the corpus and library, [the placement lookup](/tools/placement-lookup/), the technique pages, and your two cards. This is not a memory test. The question is whether you can weigh what you look up.

**On supplied questions.** The charts come with questions attached, as the old casebooks' teaching charts did. Treat each as the assigned question: decide who the querent is, write that down first, and read for that person. The advice part especially needs a person addressed.

**What is closed.** Keep the model solutions closed until the proper stage. Each reading has three stages. **Stage 1, the cast, verified** gives only structural facts. Open it after your derivation or erection is written and checked, before reading. **Stage 2, the findings to weigh** gives relevant findings and rules without an answer. Open it only if stuck, and record that you did. **Stage 3, the model reading** gives the full six-part reading. Open it only after your own reading is finished, signed, and dated. Treat it as another worked reading, not an answer key.

The model solutions are not answer keys. The scan has factual parts: perfection is present or absent, and mistakes there are mistakes. The weighing is different. It follows rules, with every weight cited. A supported divergence can outrank an unsupported agreement because the work, not the conclusion alone, is what is being graded.

**What each sitting must produce.** One written reading: the question and class, the house assignment with a one-sentence source note, the full cast or erection, the Lesson 9 chart record, the weighing with rules named where they do work, the six parts, and a grading note after Stage 3.

## Reading I: the thing hoped for

*A querent asks about a hoped-for thing that depends partly on other people's help:* **"Will the querent get what they hope for?"**

This first reading applies the method to a class no worked example has read in full. Assign the question to its house and support that assignment in one sentence. Then run the full reading procedure: verified derivation, triad and row, significators and springings, perfection scan and direction, review, refinements, weighing, and the six parts. Quote the quesited figure's Book II column and at least one parallel source. If the sources divide, resolve the disagreement in the written reading. In an unfamiliar class, read the house's chapter-head before looking at the chart; the chapter-head tells you what the house can answer.

## Reading II: the prisoner

*A man lies in prison. One who loves him asks for him:* **"Will the prisoner be released?"**

The second reading tests the reading order. Much of the chart will tempt you toward denial, but the method gives another answer if you scan before weighing. The question belongs to the twelfth house: prison, where the asker has no direct sight of the matter. Run the method in order and write each step before the next.

This chart tests role. A flat-balance reader will count many difficult findings and deny. A triad-first reader will see the Judge and deny. A lookup-only reader will read the prison column and deny. The correct method scans first, ranks before counting, and keeps each finding in its own role. Deliver the result in the six parts, with the serious-answer discipline from Lesson 11.

<SolutionStage n={3} label="The model reading">

There is conjunction: **the querent's Tristitia stands in the eleventh, beside the twelfth**, while nothing of the prison approaches the first. The chart perfects by the querent's approach. By Rule 1, the scan gives the answer: *he comes out.* Now weigh the contrary finding. **The quesited's finding:**

  <Quote id="cattan/book-2/house-12/cauda-draconis" />

  "Signifyeth **to holde a prisoner**… For all the demaundes which ye may make in this house this figure is ill." The prison's own column says *held*. That is strong finding about how the prison holds, but it is not the answer. The scan gives the answer. The triad then supports it: Acquisitio and Via yield Amissio, and the row (`cattan/book-3/witness-judge/acquisitio--via`) says **"prison: come out."** The chart is divided: the prison column says held; the scan and triad row say delivered. Rank resolves the division. Rule 1 gives whether he comes out to the scan. Rule 2 gives the triad the end and course. The column describes the resistance. **The Judge.** Amissio means loss, and Rule 9 makes the question decisive: in a prison question, loss can mean loss of the bonds. The reading is flagged as the course's application of Rule 9, supported by Amissio's first-house exception for prisoners and by the row's own *come out*. The deliverance still costs: the row says substance ill, and Puer is doubled in the second and third. Carcer on the tenth shows resistant officials. The Part of Fortune in the twelfth on Cauda makes the exit the only gain in the matter. The way of the point ends in the eighth, showing fear of the worst.

  **The six parts.** **Answer:** delivered; he comes out of prison, because the chart perfects and the triad row says *come out*. **Manner:** with difficulty and against resistance from officials. **End:** loss of the bonds, but also loss of money or estate in getting him free. **Timing:** slow, with stable figures on the asker and the officials; the exact when is a separate question. **Advice:** keep pressing the suit; the perfection is the asker's approach, so continued effort is part of the answer. Spend what must be spent, and tell the prisoner only what the chart says: he comes out, but not quickly. **Limits:** the prison column's *held* remains recorded as the main contrary finding. This reading answers release only, not guilt, charge, or life after release.
</SolutionStage>

## Reading III: one question, two systems

*A marriage is intended, and the families are asking. The question can be read in both systems:* **"Will the intended marriage go ahead?"**

The last reading asks the same question in both systems. Cast and read the shield chart first, using the full shield method and six parts. Then separately erect and read Gerard's chart on the same question, using Gerard's signs, lords, and parts, with the six-part form flagged as borrowed. Then write one concord note: *agree*, *disagree*, or *speak past each other*, and in what. Do not use a sign, lord, rule, or term from one system in the other.

Use this order. Finish and date the shield reading before reading the Gerard chart. Otherwise the second reading will be shaped by the first. This sitting tests whether you can keep two complete methods separate until both answers are sealed.

## Ongoing practice

After the third sitting, the course's work is done and practice begins. Cast real questions only, read each in full, and where both systems suit the question, read both and note whether they agree. Note each cast and, when it arrives, its outcome; readings checked against outcomes are how the skill grows from here.

## Grading yourself

Self-assessment needs a rubric because vague review is easy to distort. Do not ask "was that good?" Ask the five questions below and answer them in writing beside each sitting:

| Criterion | Passes when | Fails when |
|---|---|---|
| The cast | Your derivation or erection agrees with Stage 1 exactly, verified in writing before reading | Any figure, sign or placement differs: a miscast chart fails the cast, not the reader; recast and resit |
| The placements | Every weight assigned cites its finding: a column, a row, a technique, a part | Any sentence of the reading that cannot answer "says who?" |
| The rules | Each rule that did work is named where it worked, and no rule is made to do another's work | Weighing by instinct with citations decorating it afterward |
| The parts | All six present, each in its own role, the difficult ones said plainly | A part missing, two parts fused, or advice softening the answer |
| The systems | In Reading III and ever after: each system read wholly in its own terms, the lent form flagged | One sign, lord or rule found across the border |

Three grading rules follow. First, **a supported divergence outranks an unsupported agreement**: if your answer differs from the model reading but cites its sources and keeps the reading order, record and support the divergence; a scan error is different, and fails. Second, **Stage 2 openings are recorded, not punished** if used honestly. Third, **one resit per reading** is allowed after a day, with both attempts kept.

## You have finished this course when…

- The three readings are sat and graded by the rubric: the cast exact in all three, every divergence from the model solutions either conceded or supported in writing.
- Reading II's answer was *delivered*, or your denial is well argued and you have resat the chart and watched the reading order hold the second time.
- Reading III's two readings share a question but keep their terms separate, and its concord note is written.
- The synthesis quiz is taken from memory, checked against its key, and every miss conceded or argued.
- The Gerard card and the question map are kept for use.

## Where the practice goes

There is no next lesson. The site remains as a working library. Use the [library](/library/) for the sources, especially Cattan's worked cases and the Gerard chapters not assigned in the course. Use the [technique pages](/techniques/) during readings. Use the [figures](/figures/) and [houses](/houses/) for placement columns, and study the Witness–Judge tables slowly. The [course charts](/course/charts/) remain as standing questions to recast and compare against new charts. From here, your own readings and their outcomes are the record that matters.

The course closes with one source's own limit. Cattan, in the middle of Book III, pausing between techniques, wrote this about a use of geomancy he was about to teach:

<Quote id="cattan/book-3/ch-19-to-knowe-what-an-other-thinketh" />

"Not to the intent that any man give any credit or trust to it more then to the rest of the Arte, which was for none other purpose invented (as I have many times protested and said) but for the pastime and pleasure of the wittie heads and inventive." Treat that as the source's own limits part. Cattan wrote a long and serious method, read named cases, and still says geomancy does not demand belief and includes pleasure. Whether that statement was conviction or caution is for historians. The practical point is simple: take the method seriously, keep the flags and outcome records honestly, and do not make geomancy more solemn than the sources make it.

You have used both systems and read the hope, the prison, and the marriage. The course is finished. Practice continues as one question, one reading, and an answer that can be checked.
