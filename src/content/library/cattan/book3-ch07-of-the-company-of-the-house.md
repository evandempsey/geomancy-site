Of the company of the house.

Chap. 7.

WHen you finde a good fygure in a good house, it is double good, because the house is good and the fygure also, and signifyeth that without any doubt the querant shal obtaine his demaunde. By the like reason if yee finde an ill fygure in an ill house, it is very ill for the querant, but if ye finde a good fygure in an ill house, it signifyeth good to the querant, but it will not continue, but it taketh away some part of the malice of the house : in like case if yee finde an ill fygure in a good house, it taketh away the malice of the figure, for she would doe harme, but shee can not, keeping alwaies that the good come not to the querant : and for, as much as in this Chapter I have promised to speake of the company of fygures, I will that you doe understand that this companie is of three manners, whereof the one is simple, the other demy simple, and the third compound.

<span class="source-margin"><span class="source-margin__label">margin</span>two dot-figures side by side</span> The companie simple is of two like figures, as by example, if that yee finde *Aquisitio* in the first house, and likewise in the seconde, and so likewise of all other fygures which in two houses next together be found both of one sort, as if *Conjunctio* be found in the third, and likewise in the fourth.

<span class="source-margin"><span class="source-margin__label">margin</span>dot-figures</span> When in two houses next together, there be found two fygures a like, and that they be good, ye shall say incontinent that they signifye great goodnesse, and if they be ill, they doe signifie much ill : as by way of example, if ye finde in the fift and ninth *Rubeus*, ye shall say that it signifyeth much ill to the querant, for the question demaunded, and to declare unto you more easely, you must knowe that the second house is alwayes companion of the fyrst, the third of the fourth, the fift of the sixt, and so consequently of the others. If therefore they be both of one element, of one Planet, and of one signe, they signifye much good or much ill, according to their goodnesse or malice. If they be good they signifye that the happe and goodnesse of the querant shall be as well good present as in time to come : as much shall yee judge of the contrarie part if they be evill, and [that] because that the first house signifyeth the time present, and the second the time to come, and likewise of other companies.

<span class="source-margin"><span class="source-margin__label">margin</span>dot-figures</span> The company demie simple is, when two figures be not both of one sort, nature or condition, although they be both of one Element, and of one Planet, so as the one partie do agree, and the other not, as by example, if it happen that the first be *Aquisitio* and the second *Laticia*, although they be both of the Element, of the aire, and of the Planet [Jupiter], yet be they of diverse signifycations, for that the one of them is of [Jupiter] direct, and the exaltation of [Sun], and the other of [Jupiter] retrogade and the exaltation of [Moon] the one of the signes of [Aries], and the other of the signe of [Taurus].

<span class="source-margin"><span class="source-margin__label">margin</span>dot-figure diagram</span> The company compound is that which is of divers fygures made one contrarie to an other, as if *Aquisitio* be in the first house, and *Amissio* in the second house, of the which two commeth and is engendred the fygure *Via*, which is a figure of the element of the water, signifying a conjunction of [Sun] and [Moon], which is a triple and compound companie, evill and of great discord, by reason that *Aquisitio* is a fygure of the element of the aire, and of the Planet [Jupiter] in the signe of [Aries] *Amissio* a fygure of the element of the Fire, and of the Planet [Venus] in the signe of [Scorpio]. Which maketh and engendreth the difference of them, and the diversitie and discorde which they have together, out of the which two, as I have saide before, is engendred this fygure *Via*, which is a figure of the element of the water, and of the Planet [Moon] in the signe of [Leo], and is thus contrarie to both the others. Now see how the companie is ill, and that is the cause that when it commeth it can not be judged. And thus of all the others according to the importance of their signifycation, be it good or be it evill.

<span class="source-margin"><span class="source-margin__label">margin</span>dot-figure diagram</span> There is moreover an other companie of figures which be taken by the pointes on hie of the saide figures, as by example if that *Aquisitio* be in the first house, and *Albus* in the second, the which because they be both good fygures, and be equall of points in the upper part, and that out of them is taken an other which is *Caput draconis* likewise equall in the upper part, it is thereby signifyed that both they be of great force in things good and hoat, and that by the occasion that the fire is the first next unto the Planets, and principall element of all the other, unto whom the first points of the figures be attributed. And for that cause I have set in the fyrst booke the Chapters as well of the Fire, as of the other Elements, to the ende you may knowe their vertues and properties. As much and for the same reason, I have made a Chapter, in the which I have shewed the forme and manner to set the figures by lines, attributing the first to the Fire, as to the fyrst and superiour and principall Element of all the other, the second to the Ayre, the third to the Water, the fourth to the Earth.

A figure and example to shewe howe to judge the figures of the company by pointes.

[Here follows a ruled chart of the figure in pricked star-points: eight dot-figures in the upper row, the foure nephewes beneath, the two witnesses, and the judge at the foot.]

The Lord of Garembert of Permeran being desirous of a Ladie to be his friend, desired me on a time to make him a figure to know whether he should have his purpose pretended, unto the which to doe him pleasure I consented, and made him this figure. In the which, because that *Aquisitio* is in the first house, and hath two points on the head, and that his companion hath but one, & by that cause doe not very well agree together : but yet because they be both good figures in case of love, I judged that he should obtaine his purpose, but not without great paine and travaile, because the companie agreeth not very well. And because that the figure which commeth out of the[m], which is *Cauda draconis*, resembleth the second in the superiour pointes, which pointes be attributed unto the Fire, by that is signifyed that the partie querant shall enjoy his desire. And because *Aquisitio* is in the house of the demaundant, because he hath two pointes in the upper part, it is a fygure which doeth much participate of the Fire, rather alone then the two together as touching the companie. Because also that it is a figure of [Jupiter] in the signe of [Aries], and the exaltation of [Sun], it sheweth that the love shall be opened, whereby the mother and kinsfolkes will be very ill contented: and because *Rubeus* is in the fift house I judged that the sonne of the woman by indignation, and in an anger would goe about to kill the sayd Gentleman: and because the companie of the fift called *Leticia*, which is the sixt, is good: I sayde that the saide Gentleman should dispend much money in the sute of this woman : and because the eleventh is a fygure of [Sun] and a companion of an ill fygure, I judged that his friends should promise helpe and succour unto him, but they would not doe it untill it were to late, so that finally he should lose all his hope of tarrying for the attayning of his hearts desire. But for that the seventh is a good fygure, and attributed unto [Jupiter] as the first is, I sayde that it should be a signe that the woman should love him well, and by that meanes should in the ende marry with him in spight of her children and kindred. Which thing afterward came even so to passe, so that I ryding post with my Lorde of Thays, going to Rome, was advertised thereof and founde my fygure true, and that the Gentleman had marryed the sayde Ladie: which fygure shall serve you for an example to knowe howe to judge the Companie of figures.

A figure of marriage of the yong Ladie of Gie which is an example howe to judge the company demy simple.

[Here follows a ruled chart of the figure in pricked star-points: eight dot-figures in the upper row, the foure nephewes beneath, the two witnesses, and the judge at the foot.]

BEcause that here before I have given you an example howe to judge the figure of company by the points in a Figure of love, I will now give you another in a figure of marriage of a great lady of the kings court, which desired me upon a day to know if her marriage begunne should be accomplished, which thing I would not refuse to doe for the affection that it proceeded from, and having made the figure I judged on this sorte. First, because the first figure which is *Aquisitio*, and a figure of [Jupiter] direct in the signe of [Aries], and that hir companion which is *Latitia*, is also a figure of [Jupiter], but retrograde in the signe of [Taurus] : and by this meanes contrary to the other, and called the demy company: and moreover, because the second goeth into the eight in the company of a good figure, which is *Fortuna maior* which is in the seventh : I say for this cause, that all this doth signifie that the marriage begunne should be accomplished : and because the angles which be the first, fourth, seventh and tenth be good Figures, and do more participate of the Element of the Ayre then of the others, I judged that that was a signe, that wythout doubt it should take effect. And because that *Conjunctio* was in the fift, which signified, that the saide marriage should be finished in despite of the kindred, friends and servants, which was done in Lyons : and thus shall ye judge other questions in considering well of the figures and of their companies.
