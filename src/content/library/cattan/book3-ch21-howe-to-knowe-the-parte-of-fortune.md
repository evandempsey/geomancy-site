The question being made, after that we have judged by the houses, figures, angles, companies, aspectes, the way of point, and by all other sortes and manners before saide, nowe resteth it to judge by the parte of fortune, the which fashion of judgement is very necessarie and profitable. The part of fortune is taken on this wise, by accompting the points of the 12. figures, which afterwards ye must divide into twelve partes, and that which remaineth give unto the figures. As if there rest two ye must give it unto the second figure, if there do remaine foure to the fourth figure, if it be sixe to the sixt figure, if it bee eight to the eight, if it be ten to the tenth fygur, if it be twelve to the twelfth fygure. As by example, if the fygure be of 72. points, or 84. or 96. or of 108. points, then the parte of fortune shall goe into the twelfth. But if the saide points of fygure made, being divided by twelve, there doe remaine but two, as if there remaine seaventie and foure where there remaineth but two, then (as before we have saide) ye must give that unto the second house, and there shall be the parte of fortune. The which if the fygure and house be good (for both the one and the other must be looked uppon) you shall judge good, and if it bee evill ye shall also judge evill: and so likewise shall ye doe of all the other fygures. But if the fygure be good, and the house ill, or contrary, the house good and the fygure ill, you shall judge the saide parte of fortune to be meane. And, to the end ye may the more easier knowe the place where the fygure falleth, which is called the parte of fortune, ye shall marke it with this marke ☼, and thereafter ye shall judge all the question for the which the saide Figure was made, as ye shall see by the example that followeth.

Upon a day my Lorde of Tays minding to buy a horse, commaunded me to make a fygure, to knowe if that the horse that he should buy were good or bad: and I minding to pleasure him, as unto my lorde and maister, made for him this present figure, and because that therein the first figure and his companion be good figures, and likewise the fourth and seaventh, and the Angles for the better parte were good, I judged indifferent well, but bicause the aspects were ill, and the opposition meane, and that the way of point went into an ill fygure: and also because the parte of fortune happened on an ill fygure and house, I judged it to be ill. And because the twelfth house is the house wherein be contained the questions and demaundes which may be put touching horses and other great beasts: and as touching the members of the body, those which may be put concerning the feete and legges, and forbecause that in that place I founde

<div class="source-locator source-locator--block">p. 199</div>

an ill fygure called Amissio, I judged that the horse had an evill foote, and was worth nothing: and so it was founde true in the Towne of Lyon. Many doe use an other manner to finde part of fortune, in taking all the points as well of the twelve houses as of the two witnesses, and the Judge, which they do part by twelve (as is aforesaide) but because I have founde no trueth therein I will speake no more thereof.
