Of the Tryne aspect. Chapt. 15.

THe Tryne aspect is in the fygure, when the first, fift, and ninth fygures doe beholde one an other. And this aspect is naturally good and perfect amitie. The reason why the first, fift and ninth fygures be said to behold one an other by a Tryne aspect: the reason is because that fygures from 5. to 5. doe beholde themselves by that aspect: the which aspect as I have saide before, is of good and perfect amitie, by reason of the house, place and fygure, as by example: if that *Aquisitio* which is a good fygure, be in the first, fyft and ninth houses: or *Fortuna maior*, or *Laeticia* in the saide fift or ninth houses, *Aquisitio* being in the first, which is a very good signe for the querant, for they be all of the Element of the Ayre. As often therefore as you finde a fygure in the fyrst house, and that it goe thence into the fyft or ninth houses, you shall say the Tryne aspect is good and perfect, and signifyeth good happe to the querant, and good issue of his demaund, and if the fygures be all of one Element, it is the better. If yee finde a fygure entring and good in the fyft or ninth houses which passeth from one house to an other, although the fyrst be not like unto it, yet shall the signifycation be of good fortune to the querant, for the practise of which things, I will give you the example following.

The Cardinall Trivulsie, being come Legate into France to entreate of peace betweene the King and the Emperour, I was desired by a Gentleman my friend, to make a fygure to know whether the peace should be or not, and I made unto him this fygure here above: and in the same because that the first fygure is *Coniunctio*, which goeth into the third house, it signifyeth thereby that they should treate of this peace, but because the first is beheld with an ill Aspect fro[m] the fift which is a fygure of ♄ retrograde, called *Tristitia*, it signifyed that there should bee no peace made at this time. But for as much as the ninth is a good fygure, which is the house of Churchmen, it is a signe that the sayde Cardinall would doe what he were able to conclude this peace, but because the seventh is an ill fygure, I judged thereby that the Emperour should have no peace. Also for that the regarde Sextile, of the eleventh fygure is ill. And because the fift, which is the Tryne Aspect, is a fygure of ♄, and that the witnesses and Judge be all of the same Planet. I judged thereby that they should make no peace, and because the second is gone into the eight, which is the house of the substance of the Emperour, I saide that the king should loose much of his substance. But because the Trine Aspect of the eight to the twelfth is of an ill fygure, it signifyeth that the Emperour should not long keepe the substance which he should winne, and so it came to passe afterward, according to the judgement which I made of this same fygure. And whilest I made the fygure, the king and the Cardinall were at Lyons: and shortly after the Emperour tooke Provaunce untill Ayre, where he stayed not long, but departed, and lost all that he had taken: and so my fygure was true. And this is an example to judge a fygure by the Tryne Aspect, which is a very necessarie rule to judge a fygure well.
