1 The fift house called the succedant of the angle of the septentrionall, otherwise called the good fortune, doth containe properly the significations of the demaundes which may be made touching a childe, to knowe whether he shall be small or great of stature, and touching his birth, whether he be legitimate or bastarde, of good nature or ill.

2 The demaundes likewise which may be moved on the maner of his living, that is to say, if it be profitable to a person to eate and drincke.

3 Whether it be good to take newe clothes, and whether the person be well apparelled.

4 If a promise made to a person shall be performed or not, and whether it be false or true.

5 Whether the messenger shall come quickely, and what newes hee shall bring, and also what is contained in the letters.

6 Also the demaundes which may be made uppon a friend, man or woman.

7 If the earth shall bring forth plenty of fruites, and whether they shall be good.

8 Likewise the significations of all pleasures, as to kisse, coll, sing, daunce, banquet, and play on all instruments of musicke.

9 Whether a woman be with childe or not, and whether she shall have a boy or wench.

10 If that a place beseeged shalbe taken or yeelded by composition, or whether it hath beene beseeged.

11 Whether the person which is on the way shall be in danger to be robbed and spoiled, and whether the wayes be not dangerous by reason of theeves, ruffians, and such like.

12 If the sonne be sicke, dead, or taken prisoner.

13 Whether the Booke which one would reade containe things good or bad. These be the principall causes contained under this house, the which yee shall judge according unto the rules and significations of the Figures hereafter written in the order as before.

## Aquisitio — house 5

When in this fift house you fynd this Figure called Aquisitio, it giveth good significations of the childe, and that he shall be of good wit and condition, and well made of body, the messenger shall come quickely, with good news: in case to live, and to have newe clothes it is good, the promise shall be kepte in all faithfulnesse, the letters speake not but of merry things, and recreative as of love, or of matter beneficiall, the amitie of the friends is good, it is also good for the fruites of the earth, and there shall be great abundance, it is good and sure dwelling in the house, the woman with childe shall have a senne, so that the tenth do consent to this, it is good to kisse, coll, dally, dance, and to doe al things of mirth and pleasure, the Commons of the towne be good, and live in al peace and quietnesse, the Castell is not beseeged, and if it be, it shall not be taken, by reason of the good accorde amongest them which be within it: there is neither theeves, neither roisters by the way, the sonne is neyther dead, sicke, or taken prisoner, but maketh good cheere, and is at his pleasure, the friend is merry, and the childe legitimate, Bookes doe containe Church matters and merinesse, al things which yee may demaunde in this house this figure dooth signifye good, and also that a woman shall not have manie children.

## Amissio — house 5

Finding in this fift house the Figure called Amissio, it signifyeth that the childe shal be of ill nature, and losse of heretage by lawe, the sicke person shall amend, the woman with childe shal have a sonne, but she shal be in danger of death, it is not good in case of eating and drincking, the promise shal not be performed, the messenger shall come quickely, but he shall bring ill newes, the letters speake of quarrels or else of ribauldrie, the friends be not too good or jocunde, for pleasure and jocundnesse this figure is ill, unlesse it be for wantonnes and baudery, for the which it is good, the dwelling in the house is ill and dangerous, the Commons of the City be bad, if the Castell bee beseeged, it shal be taken, vagabonds lie by the way, and therefore it is dangerous, the son is neither dead, nor taken prisoner, but he is very sicke, the childe is a bastard, to buy and sell newe garments it is ill, the yeare is good, and great aboundaunce of corne, but it is ill for trees and plants: the bookes and writings make mention of ribauldry, or of questions. For all the things which ye may demaund in this house this figure is ill, and the woman shall have many children, but they shall be ill.

## Fortuna maior — house 5

When ye finde in this fift house this figure called Fortuna maior, it is a signe of fewe children, but they shall be honest and happy in their life: the sonne is neither sicke neither dead, but shall come quickely, it is good to eate and drincke, the promise shall be kept, the messenger is not stayed, neither shall it be long before he returne and bring good tidings, and the letters which he bringeth speake of the affaires of Kings, Princes, and greate Lordes, and of all things pleasant and recreative, thy friend is good and trusty: it is good and holsome to dwell in the house, it is good to kisse, coll, daunce, and to make all pretie pastimes, the woman shall have a sonne wythout any danger of death, so that the first agree thereunto: there will be raine if the tenth do agree, the commons of the Towne is good, and there is much nobilitie therein: the Castell is not beseeged, there is no vagabondes in the way, the childe is legitimate, it is good to buy and put on newe clothes: the yeare shall be good, and great aboundance of all the fruites on earth: the bookes written doe speake of kings and Princes, and of their victories: in all the thinges which ye may demaunde in this house the figure is good.

## Fortuna minor — house 5

If you finde in this fift house the figure called Fortuna minor, it signifyeth, that the childe shall be a man of honour, victorious over his ennemies and liberall, and he shall have manie children: it is indifferent good in eating and drincking, the promise shall take place, the messenger shall returne quickely, and the newes which he shall bring shall be of warre or of anger: it is not good for the friend but onely in ribauldry. Touching pleasures, there is small joy or hearts ease, it is ill to remaine in the house, unlesse it be for matters venereall: the woman wyth child shall have a daughter, and escape death very narrowly, the commons of the towne is ill, if the Castell be beseeged it shall be taken, there is many theeves by the way, and therefore it is not good to goe into the Countrey: the sonne is sicke, but hee shall escape the disease, and come home quickely, so that the tenth doe agree thereto: the childe is a bastarde, it is ill to buy and put on newe clothes, for they shall endure but a while: the yeare shall be good and plentiful, but ill for trees, the books treate of warre or of anger, or of great fury: for all the things which you may demaunde in this house this figure signifyeth good, and also to have but fewe children.

## Letitia — house 5

Finding in this fift house the figure called Letitia, it dooth signifie prosperity, good lucke, and the advauncement of honour to the childe by his good nature, the woman with childe shall have a sonne which shal have great honour and reputation in his life: for al the demaundes which ye can demaunde in this house this figure is good, as to buy and put on newe clothes, and for the plentifulnes and fruitfulnes of the yeere: and consequently this figure is good in al the demaunds even as it is called Lætitia. which is called the house of Joy, wherefore at this time I wil say no other thing, but that it signifyeth to have few children.

## Tristitia — house 5

If you finde in the fift house this figure called Tristitia, it signifyeth sorrowe, heavines, unquietnes, poverty, and ill fortune hereafter to come to the childe, the Letters which come speake of heavy things, as of a towne beseeged, or of other mishappes and inconveniences, the woman wyth childe shal have a sonne (so that the tenth doe give consent) and she shall have a great and long travel wyth danger of death: to be briefe, in al the demaundes which you may make in this house this figure signifyeth ill fortune, and is therefore here called Tristitia, the which is sadnes, quite contrary to the house called the house of Joy, and this figure signifieth to have many children.

## Puella — house 5

When in this fift house ye finde the figure called Puella, it signifieth prosperity to the childe, it is also good to eate and to drinke, and for all mirth and pleasure, the promise shal be kept, the messenger shal come something late, but he shall bring good and perfite newes which shal speake of love, it is good for the friend, and for all mirth, as to kisse, coll, daunce, sing and play on instruments of musicke, the childe is a bastarde, there is no vagabonds by the way, the Castel is not beseeged: as touching the woman wyth childe some say she shall have a boy, and some a wench: and forbecause that in this matter the Doctours in this Arte doe not agree, to be more certaine you must have recourse to the Angles of the figure, by the which you shall judge as they speake: to buy and put on newe clothes it is good, and signifyeth that they shall be rich, neate, and fine, the yeare shall be good, and great store of all sortes of good things: finally, the Bookes speake of kissing, colling, dauncing, and singing: wherefore when ye finde this figure in this house, you shall judge, as is aforesaide, and further say that it signifyeth fewe children, but they shal be faire.

## Puer — house 5

Finding this Figure called Puer in the fift house, it signifyeth the childe to bee of ill conversation, and disobedient unto father and mother: for governement of eating and drincking it is ill, the promise shall not be performed, the messenger will not stay long before he come and bring letters, which make mention of warre: it is not good for a friend, nor for pastime, but in seats of warre, the dwelling in the house is not good: it is worth nothing to kisse, coll, or such like dalliance, but in the venereall actes: the woman with childe shall have a sonne, the place beseeged shall not be taken, but defend it selfe valiantly: there bee many theeves lying in waite by the way, the sonne is not dead, but hurte, the childe is a bastard, ill to buy and put on newe clothes or other things unlesse it be harnes and armour, for the which it is good: the yeare shall be indifferent good, saving that there shall be some haile: the Bookes speake of warre, because this is a figure of ♂ which is lorde of warre, it signifieth likewise fewe children, and they shall be all men of warre, and pillers of poore men and laborers of the field.

## Rubeus — house 5

In finding in the fift house this figure called Rubeus, it signifyeth perverse children, and degenerate from vertue: it is ill touching eating and drinking, and especially of redde things, the promise shal not be kept, the messenger shall bring letters which shall make mention of warres and combates. And to be briefe this figure hath no signification in this house but ill (excepte to sowe the ground) for the which it is good: the woman wyth childe shall have a daughter, because that this figure is a figure of ♂ R: and moreover, if that there be any children, they shall not live but die quickly.

## Albus — house 5

If you finde this figure called Albus in the fift house, it signifyeth that the childe shall be of good nature and complexion, it is good to eate and drinke, and health shal come thereof, the promisse shall take good effect, the messenger shal come quickely wyth good newes, the letters make mention of things mercuriall and white. As often as in making a figure you finde this saide figure in the fift house, be ye assured that you shall receive letters from one or other within three dayes: it is good for a friend, and likewise for mirth and joy, it is good for the dwelling in a house: it is good for to kisse and coll in the waie of honestie, but ill for dishonestie, the woman with childe shall have a sonne, without any danger of death, or any other inconvenience: the commons of the Citie be good, the Castell is not beseeged, but if it be it shall be rendred by composition: there is no harlots or ill folkes by the way, the childe is not dead, the childe is legitimate, it is good to buy and put on newe clothes, and above all other, white clothes: it shall be a good and fruitefull yeare, the bookes speake of great and profound knowledge: and because it is a Figure of ☿ it seemeth to have manie children.

## Coniunctio — house 5

When ye fynde this Figure called Coniunctio, in the fifte house, it signifyeth that the childe shal be of an ill nature: and to tel you in two wordes, there is no demaunde that may bee made in this house, but that this figure is meane therein: the woman with childe shall have a sonne: and when soever yee do make a Figure for a marriage, and fynde this Figure in the fifte house, be ye sure that the marriage shall take effect. At this time I will say no more of this Figure, but that it signifyeth to have fewe children or none, but if there be any, they can not live long.

## Caput draconis — house 5

Finding this figure named Caput draconis in the fift house, it signifyeth, that the children shall be of great wisedome, and honour, and gentle to al folkes, it is profitable to a person to eate and drinke well, the promise shall be performed, the messenger will tarry long, but yet he bringeth good newes, the letters speake of love or of riches: it is good for a friend, and likewise for venereall workes and mirth, and to dwell still in a house: the woman with childe shall have a sonne wythout any danger of death, the commons of the Citie be good, the castel is not beseeged, there are no ruffians on the way, the sonne is not dead, but wil come quickely, the sonne is legittimate: it is good to buy and put on newe clothes, the yeare shal be plentiful of all things, the Bookes and writings doe speake parte of love, and part of Church matters, and of importance. This figure is very good in all the demaunds, and sheweth to have but fewe children, but they shal be wise and good.

## Cauda draconis — house 5

When in the fift house ye finde this Figure called Cauda draconis, it signifyeth that the childe shall be wicked, and in the ende be hanged, or die some evil death, or banished or exiled the Countrey: it is ill to eate and drinke, the messenger shal come quickely, but his newes shall be evill, and the letters speake of warre and of treason: the promise shal be nothing kept, it is ill for a friend, and likewise for mirth or joy, unlesse it be in wars, in which it is good, it is ill to dwell in that house for it shal be burned or overthrowen: the woman with childe shal have a daughter, and be in great danger of death, the communaltie of the cittie be al theeves and wicked, the castell beseeged shal bee burned, taken, and ransaked: there is much ill company by the way, the sonne is dead or prisoner, the childe is a bastard: it is ill to buy or put on any newe garmentes, except it be harnesse, the yeare shal be plenteous in corne, but not in fruites, the writings and Bookes make mention of warres, and of thinges odious and sorrowful: in al the demaundes which may be made in this house this Figure is ill, and signifieth to have fewe children, and they shal be evil, and workers wyth fyre.

## Carcer — house 5

When in the fift house ye finde this Figure called Carcer, it signifyeth that the child is of a melancholy nature, saturnian, ful of grose humours, a man of travell and paine, it is not good to eate and drinke, the promise shal be performed, but it shal bee long first, the messenger shal come safe and sounde, but hee shal tary long, and be in danger to be held prisoner, the letters make mention of imprisonment, or of women with childe, or of some sad and melancholy cause: for al kind of mirth it is ill, it signifieth that the house where the habitation is is darke, smokie, and ill cast: it is ill to daunce, sing, kisse or coll: the woman with childe shal have a daughter, the commons of the towne be most olde folke, the castel beseeged shal not be taken for the good resistance which they within doe make: the sonne is not dead or in prison, the childe is legitimate: to buy and put on newe clothes it is good, for they shal last long, especialy those which be blacke: the yeare shal be indifferent good, the writings and bookes treate of women wyth childe, of prisonment, or to sowe the earth, or of things melancholy. Whensoever you make a Figure for any demaund, and finde this Figure in this place, you shal bee sure to receive some letters before it be three dayes: in al other thinges this Figure is ill, and signifyeth to have manie children.

## Populus — house 5

Finding this Figure called Populus in the fift house, it signifieth many children and wittie: it is more wholesome for the body to drinke then to eate: the promise shal be scarcely performed, the messenger shal come quickely, the woman wyth childe shal have a sonne: for a friend, and for merinesse it is good, the Letters which shal come shal speake of sundry kindes of people, as of marriners, and al other things concerning water: the yere shal be good, and there shal be abundance of al things: the sonne is not dead, but shal come quickely: it is evill to buy and put on newe clothes, for they shall not last long, the bookes and letters do speake of the sea or of some rivers. And whensoever you do make a Figure for any demaund, and finde this figure in this house, it dooth signifie to have some raine shortely: it dooth signifie to have many children, but they shall not live verie long.

## Via — house 5

When in this fift house you finde this figure Via, it dooth signifie to have fewe children, but they shall be of good nature and complexion, and not long of life: it is better to drinke then to eate, the promise shall not be kept, the woman is not with childe, but hath some lumpe in her. In all the demaundes that ye may propound this figure is ill, unlesse it be for voyages. And whensoever ye make a figure for any demaunde, and finde this figure in this place it signifieth that Letters shall come quickly from some place, the which shall speake of thinges of water, or of voyage: it doth shewe also that there shal not bee manie children, and if so be that there be any, they shall not live long.
