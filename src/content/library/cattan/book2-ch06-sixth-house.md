1 The sixt house called the Cadant from the Angle septentrionall, or the ill fortune, contayneth properly all the demaundes which may be made touching servaunts and other subjects.

2 Also the demaundes which may be made upon sicke persons and diseases, whether it shall be long or short, and of which of the foure humours it commeth, and if the patient shall be quickly hoale, or lose or destroy any of his lymmes by that diseases, and if he shall die in what estate shall he die, in good or bad, and in what day and in what houre.

3 Whether the Physician ye would goe unto be learned in his art, and if it be good to take phisicke.

4 If it be good to visite a sicke person.

5 This house contayneth also the questions which may be made on the place where the sicke person is, as the Hospitall or chamber where he is.

6 Moreover the demaundes which may be made uppon small beastes: as sheepe, lambes, goates, swyne and such like small beastes: and whether it be good to buy or sell them.

7 Whether the beastes lost shall not be found, and who is the theefe.

8 It comprehendeth likewise the demaundes which may be made upon men of lowe condition and estate: as Labourers, Masons, Carpenters, Butchers, and other Crafts-men and Porters.

9 Also over all things stroyed and broken, false witnesses, and bawdes, Sorcerers and Enchaunters.

10 Also over feare and fright, shame, povertie and lacke, smoke and darkenesse.

11 As touching the members of man, it contayneth the demaundes which may be made upon all the noble partes of the bodie, the heart excepted: these be the principall demaundes whose significations be contayned in the sixt house, as ye shall finde by each figure which hereafter be placed according to the former order.

## [Figure unnamed in text; marginal woodcut = Aquisitio] — house 6

When ye finde this figure in the sixt house, it signifyeth fidelitie and wisedome in servants, the man is not sicke, but if he be he shall mende quickly: the crafts man or labourer is an honest man and a painefull, and that which he doth is well done: the Physitian is a good man: it is good to take phisicke, so that the fift house be good: the man shall never fall into povertie. To be briefe, in all things which yee may demaunde in this house, this figure is good.

## Amissio — house 6

Finding this figure called Amissio in this house, it signifyeth ill issue and disloyaltie for all things which ye may demaund in this house touching servaunts and subjects: for the sicke person it signifieth sometime death, and sometime amendment: wherefore ye shall judge according to the first and eight houses: the sicke person is in a poore place, the sicknesse is onely of cholar, and he is so poore that he hath not a shooe to his foote: the witnesses be false: it is ill to take counsayle of the Physitian or to take physicke: you must judge according to the fift & seventh houses. It is not good to buy small beastes, for they shall all perish. And to be short, that which this figure concerneth it is worth nothing for any thing in this house, but for bawdes, for which it is good, signifying that the persons be shamelesse, and apt to that occupation.

## [Figure unnamed in text; marginal woodcut = Fortuna maior] — house 6

When in the sixt house ye finde this figure, it signifyeth that the servants and subjects be faithfull and obedient: it is good to buy beasts: the person is not dead, but if hee be sicke, hee shall quickly amende, and the disease doth come of too much aboundance and corruption of bloud: the witnesses be not false: it is good to take medecine, and the Physitian is a good man. And to be short, ye can demaunde no question in this house wherein this figure is ill, but for bawdes, whereunto it is ill because their fact shall be disclosed, and in daunger to have the bastionado, or else that worse is, to be whipt at their comming.

## Fortuna minor — house 6

When in the sixt house ye finde this figure called Fortuna minor, it signifieth that the servants and subjectes be true unto their maister: but the servants shall be sicke in their service: it is ill to buy beastes, for there will be but small profite by them: the witnesses be not false, but wil by their disposition use some spitefulnes. For the Physitian, and to take that which he ordeyneth it is good, so that the fift and seventh house doe consent: the beast lost shall never be found: it is good for whores and baudes, but they shall be in daunger to be destroyed by their suddennesse and feare, that they shall not have the money which was to the[m] promised. In all other thinges this figure is meane.

## Leticia — house 6

If in the sixt house ye finde this figure Leticia, it signifieth the servantes to be good at worke, sure and faithfull: it is good to buy beastes: the patient shall have none other diseases, but shall quickly amende: sometime the sicknesse commeth by over much rest or pensivenesse for some thing: in all other thinges which belong unto this house, this figure is good, except for baudes, shewing that they shall be false, and that their promise made shall take no place.

## Tristitia — house 6

Finding this figure Tristitia in the sixt house, it doth signifie disobedience and naughtinesse in servants and subjects, and that they shall be slothfull and sickly in their service: the sicke person shall die so that the viij. doe consent, the witnesses have falsely deposed: it is ill to buy beastes: for the Phisitian, or to take medicine, & in all things which ye may demand in this house, this figure is ill, but for baudes, for their matters will goe well.

## Puella — house 6

When in this sixt house ye finde this figure Puella, it is very good for all the demaundes which may be made: touching servantes it is ill: for the sicke person and his disease come only by phleme and thought taking for women: for all other thinges which ye may demaunde in this house, this figure is good, especially for whores and bawdes, for their affaires shall prosper: it is also profitable to buy beastes.

## Puer — house 6

You finding in the sixt house this figure Puer, it signifieth that the servants and subjectes be naught, and will robbe their maisters, it is ill for the sicke person, especially if the first house consent, it is likewise ill for small beastes, for they shall be stolen, the sicke person shall be worse and worse, and fall into worser sicknesse: in all the other questions in this house this figure is ill, but for whores and bawdes, and to rayse men to goe to warre it is good: it is likewise good to take medicine, and especially by drinke.

## Rubeus — house 6

Finding in this sixt house this figure Rubeus, it signifyeth no good lucke touching servants, for they shall be in daunger to be robbed or devoured by wolfes: the disease commeth of too much aboundance & corruption of bloud mixed with red choler: the sicke person shall die or be long sicke, if that the first and eight houses doe agree: in all the demaundes which yee may make in this house, this figure is ill, unlesse it be to lett a person bloud.

## Albus — house 6

When in the sixt house ye finde this figure Albus, it signifieth good lucke, as well for servantes as for cattell: the sickenes shall not last long: sometime this figure signifieth good, and sometime ill, according as the Angles do speake: it is good both for the Physitian, and also to take medicine: the disease commeth of phleme: in all other thinges which ye may aske in this house this figure is good, especially for company of servauntes, shewing that they shall be faithfull.

## Coniunctio — house 6

If in the sixt house ye finde this figure Coniunctio, it signifieth that the servantes be meetely good: the sicke person shall die: it is ill to take phisicke, and likewise ill for the Physitian: it is excellent good for whores and baudes: in all other demaundes this figure is ill, but to buy small cattell, whereunto it is good.

## Caput Draconis — house 6

Finding his figure Caput Draconis in the sixt house, it signifieth good luke in beastes, the sicknesse shall last long if the viij. consent thereunto: it is good for the Physitian, and also to take medicine: in all other things which ye may aske in this house, this figures good, but for whores and baudes it is ill, for their matter shal not goe well.

## Cauda Draconis — house 6

Whe[n] ye finde this figure Cauda Draconis in the sixt house, it signifie[th] that the servantes be not very good, the beastes shall be stolen or eaten by Wolves: the partie shall have a great hoat feve[r] and be in daunger of death if the viij. doe consent: it is ill as sell for the Phisitian, as also to take medicine, and for all other thinges, saving for whores and baudes, for the which it is good.

## Carcer — house 6

Whe[n] in the sixt house ye finde this figure Carcer, it signifieth disloyaltie and small securitie in servants, and small profite to buy bestes: the sicknesse shall be long and the patient in daunger of death: it is not good to take medecine: the Phisitian is n[o]t of the best learned. In all the demaunds which may be made i[n] this house this figure is ill, but it is good for whores and baudes.

## Populus — house 6

Whe[n] ye finde this figure Populus in the sixt house, it doth signifie [w]isedome and fidelitie in servantes, good to buy small beastes a[n]d sell them: it is meetely good for the sicke person: the disease co[m]meth of a phleme like unto a runne, but the patient shall ame[n]de, if the viij. give consent thereunto: it is good for the Phisitian and to take medicine. And to be short, in all the demaundes of this house, this figure is good: but it is not very good for whores and baudes for they shall be beaten: this figure is good to rayse up souldiers and to muster, and sheweth that there shall be a great number.

## Via — house 6

When in this house ye finde the figure Via it signifyeth that the servantes will willingly put foorth themselves in their maisters businesse, and doe it with great diligence: it is ill to buye and sell cattell: the sicke person shall be in daunger of death, if the viij. consent thereunto: it is ill for the Phisitian, or to take medicine: the witnesses have falsely deposed: the baude doth but mocke and will not doe her endevour, where she is woorthie to have ten thousand boxes with the fist for her hyre.
