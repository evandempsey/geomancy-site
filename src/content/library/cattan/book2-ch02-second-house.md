1 The second house called the succidant of the Angle of the Orient, hath properly and naturally the signification of gaine to come, for the person which demaundeth and propoundeth the question, to knowe if it shall be with the travaile and sweate of his bodie, or by succession, or by guift.

2 This house also contayneth the resolution of demaundes which may be made for moveable goods, which be in the power of the person demaunding, or for whom the question is propounded.

3 Which of the two gamsters hath wonne, or shall winne the silver.

4 The charges that a person shall make.

5 Whether the friend put in trust, be secret or not.

6 If he shall be well served and to his profite and commoditie, by eyther man or woman that hee mindeth to take into his service.

7 Whether he shall have great gaine in the place where he dwelleth, or of the thing that he loveth, and that he procureth.

8 The place where the thing lost was stolen.

9 If the voyage that he would take in hande, shall be profitable, and whether it be nie at hand or farre off.

10 Whether the promise made by any Lord shall come unto effect and good issue.

11 If it be good in going unto one house to passe to another.

12 Whether the messenger which is on the way shal bring good newes or how.

13 As touching the members of man it contayneth the demaundes which may be moved over the necke eyther before or behinde.

This house is properly the house of gaine and profite, and therefore when ye finde any of this xvi. figures in this seconde house, yee shall judge according to the signification of ech of them as ye shall see hereafter.

## Aquisitio — house 2

When ye finde this figure called Aquisitio, in the second you shall judge the demaundes and questions before spoken of to bee good, as when the question is for gaine or profite, you shal judge it to be good and great: and that the person shall be lucklye in quantity of Cattel, in prosperitie in traficques and merchandize, and have good successe in all thinges in the worlde that he taketh in hande. This figure in this house is better then all the others, except Fortuna major, which in this behalfe doth nothing emperish, and signifying more then the other, gaine, profite, and honour with Kings, Princes and great Lordes, and signifieth as much in matter beneficiall, and estates of Presidents and Counsellers.

## Amissio — house 2

Finding this figure called Amissio, in this second house, it is a token of losse and small gaine or profite in all thinges that you can demaunde, but to obtaine the friendship of a Ladie, as touching the way it doth signifie that the messenger shall be robbed and spoyled by a number of theeves and hedge walkers, so that in all things this figure is ill, be it for warre or for peace, and is good for nothing but for love.

## Fortuna major — house 2

This figure called Fortuna major, found in this second house, signifieth goodnesse and prosperitie, with honour, riches, great presents of gold and silver, and she is good in any question that ye can demaund, but in heavy and sad things, wherein she signifieth melancholinesse, in all other things she doth alwayes signifie, joy and blisse: in demaundes of treasons and robberies, she is good because she signifyeth loyaltie in what house so ever she be. In the house of friendes and enemies (as ye shall hereafter see) ye shall alwayes finde her to signifye force, accompanyed with loyaltie: likewise she signifyeth faire and rich attire.

## Fortuna minor — house 2

Finding this figure called Fortuna minor in the second house, signifyeth the honour past, great riches and great substance, she is good for merchandize, honors, warres and substance: it signifyeth a readinesse and quicknesse, as well for the way as for all other things, but she is not so good and loyall as that which went last before.

## Leticia — house 2

When ye finde this figure called Leticia in the second house, it signifyeth conquest and getting of goods and riches without sinne or breach of conscience, it doth signifie good companie, merry, recreative and of good will, promising many things which will not be perfourmed, for journey it signifyeth quicknesse, of all the orientall figures she is the best in all things, and in case of robberie the thing is taken away in pastime and not in earnest.

## Tristitia — house 2

Finding this figure called Tristitia, in the second house it signifyeth losse and hindrance where gaine is hoped, never to attaine unto substance, but by extreame labour, envie, and anger, accompanyed with ill lucke and misfortune, to lose goods, to be spoyled, robbed, overthrowen, and to goe in theeves companie, slowe for journeyes and small profite. This fygure is ill in all things but to fortifie Townes, and make buildings, and signifyeth that the building which ye make shall not be very faire, but it shall last long.

## Puella — house 2

When ye finde this figure called Puella, in this second house, it signifyeth good gaine, prosperitie and good lucke, and in case of women loyaltie, and virginitie, good and sure company by the way, gaine in things of pleasure and mirth: as musicke and such like, it is good in all thinges and especially in matters of silver and white things, it is also good for the way, but that there will be some stay or hinderance by the way: she signifyeth no warre but rather peace and concord.

## Puer — house 2

Finding this figure called Puer in the second house, it signifyeth profite in merchandice by occasion of a woman, and signifyeth likewise that the gaine shall be good in the warre, and that therein shall be gotten honour and profite, for companie in the way they shall be men of warre, valiant and hardy: in case of messages it signifyeth speedinesse with small profite, unlesse it be touching warre, in thinges concerning honour it is very good, touching a thing stolen it shall be had againe, but not without vexation and anger.

## Rubeus — house 2

When in the second house ye find this Figure called Rubeus which is a figure of ♂ and evill, it alwayes doth signifye small gaine and profite, and signifyeth that the person shall be robbed and spoyled, be it by warre or otherwise, it also signifyeth much debate and contention about the thing lost and stolen, and as touching company by the way, it sheweth that they be men of warre, ruffins and ryoters, to be briefe, this figure is ill in all things.

## Albus — house 2

If ye finde in the second house this figure called Albus, it signifieth great gaine and profite with honour, spirite and diligence, specially in whit things: it is good in all things, for that she signifieth a good spirite and understanding, good company, and trustie, and men of honour: she is also good in case of voyage, yet will there be some staying and hinderance therein. I can say to you none other thing of this figure, but that it is good in al the demaundes.

## Conjunctio — house 2

Finding in the second house this figure called Conjunctio, ye shall say it signifieth gaine, in things of good industry and spirite, and signifieth, that the thing lost shal be recovered againe, but not without great paine and travel, and long space of time, the company is good and of good spirit, sometime it signifieth bastards, and the obtaining of Bookes and Sciences written by hand, as touching messages, it signifieth quicke arrivall, and also getting of goodes and heretages of folkes lately dead. This figure is commonly good.

## Caput draconis — house 2

When ye finde in the second house this figure called Caput draconis, it signifieth great substance and riches, great goodnes, good company of men of honesty, the thing lost shall be founde, prosperitie and good lucke in merchandise, with great gaine: it signifieth also, that the profite which a person shall have shall be with peace and tranquilitie, without warre or debate. Also it is a good figure.

## Cauda draconis — house 2

Finding this Figure called Cauda draconis in the second house, it signifyeth all wretchednes and poverty, and that a person shall be destroyed, wasted, and undone, to sell goodes and heretages, to spend golde and silver without profite, things lost shal never be recovered, the company is nothing worth, by reason of the robberies and extortions which they use, and they be craftsmen, working by yron, as horse-showers, locke-smiths, and such like.

## Carcer — house 2

When ye finde this Figure named Carcer in the seconde house, it signifyeth gaine by things hidden in the earth with covetousnes, and things blacke: the person shall happen wyth melancholy and sad company, and of smal purpose: in case of voiage and in all other things, she signifyeth slownesse, but to build houses and fortresses she is good, but the worke shal be homely: it is good in blacke things, but it is ill in all other things.

## Populus — house 2

If ye finde this Figure called Populus in the second house, it signifyeth a quantity of men assembled for travel and merchandise, and to get substance, it signifyeth also white things, and to spend well, good company, good for voiage, and signifyeth swiftnesse, and likewise for warre, for it is a token of a great multitude of people assembled ready to fight, it is good for marriage, but colde in matter of love, it is also good to gaine and profite by the water.

## Via — house 2

Finding this figure named Via in the second house, it signifyeth small gaine, and povertie, the thing lost shall never be found againe, the company is poore, and the profite is not great: in case of marriage it is not very good unlesse the ninth do consent thereunto: it is but meetely in all things, but for journeying, for the which it is good, saving that there shall be some slackenesse therein.
