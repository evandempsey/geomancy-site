The Method of Judging Questions According to Peter of Abano, the Paduan.

I have desired to give a true and certain judgment [according to] the holy, glorious, and venerable science of Geomancy. First, one ought to invoke the clemency of Almighty God, from whom all good things have their origin, to supplicate and pray that he may express the true signification of the figures, and open the secrets of the twelve houses, and of the Judge with the Witnesses, by a true path. In this judgment, nine things are to be considered.

First: the goodness and malice of the figures.

Second: that they know who the significators of that question are.

Third: if the first sign, which signifies the querent, occupies the house of the thing questioned (Occupation).

Fourth: if the first significator seeks conjunction with the other significator, or vice versa; if the significator of the questioned thing seeks conjunction with the querent.

Fifth: to see whether mutation occurs in the question—that is, if both significators move outside their own places, making a conjunction between themselves (Mutation).

## PDF p. 461 / ms p. 222v

Sixth: to see if there is any translation [of light/disposition] between the significators (Translation).

Seventh: the goodness or malice of the Witnesses and the Judge is to be considered.

Eighth: the nature and signification of the sixteenth figure is to be considered.

Ninth: whether there will be a swift arrival, or the cutting off of hope for a lost thing.

### On the Goodness and Malice of the Figures

The goodness or malice of the figures is considered in this manner. For one must consider the goodness or malice of the four angles (cardinals), namely, whether the figures are benevolent. For if the first is benevolent, a good beginning or the goodness of the querent is to be presumed, and vice versa. If the fourth is good, one should rejoice in a good end. If the seventh and tenth are good, one may hope for a good middle, and vice versa. And if the fifteenth [the Judge] strengthens the fourth, there is no doubt of an excellent end; all of which is the opposite [if they are evil].

### On the Aspects of the Figures

Next, it must be seen whether good figures are in the aspects of the ascendant; for if they are in sextile aspect, that is in the 3rd and 11th houses, it is good; if in trine aspect, that is in the 5th and 9th houses, it signifies better; all of which is the opposite [if evil figures are in aspect], since the ascendant is thereby weakened.

### On the Joys of the Figures

Next, the joys of the figures are to be considered. For the figures of Mercury ☿ ☿ rejoice in the ascendant [1st house]; the figures of the Moon ☽ ☽ in the third house; of Venus ♀ ♀ in the fifth house; of Mars ♂ ♂ in the sixth house; of the Sun ☉ ☉ in the ninth house; of Jupiter ♃ ♃ in the eleventh house; of Saturn ♄ ♄ in the twelfth. And when a good figure is in its joy, it duplicates its good signification. If it is an evil figure, its malice is duplicated.

### On the Society of the Figures

Likewise, it must be seen which companion figure it uses and from which it arises. For if a good figure has a good companion, its good signification is completed and increased, and vice versa. If it has a good companion: moreover, if a good figure is procreated from good parents [i.e., the figures from which it is generated], it signifies a full good; and if from one good and another evil, it remains in its own proper signification. And if it arises from two evil ones, it is infected by them and remains temperate; if it should be an evil figure, the whole is vice versa.

### On the Mutation of a Figure and Its Signification

It should be known that a figure can change its fortune. This signifies what happens when fortunate signs exert their signification against the will and hope of the querent, resulting in the negation of the arrival of the desired thing. For example: if someone asks whether rain is coming, and a figure is formed in the 10th house, which is the house of rain or dryness; if this figure signifies fair weather, and rain is loss [amissio], it changes its fortune: what would normally be a good figure becomes an evil signification. The weather remains fair, since this is against the desire and will of the querent. For every figure that must signify contrary to the will of the querent, whatever it may be, judge it as evil to that extent in that question, and vice versa.

### On Knowing How to Find the Significator

Since the judgment of Geomancy in no way seems possible to be given if the artist is ignorant of the houses of the question and the significators, by whose significations the arrival or transaction of the questioned thing is known. For the significators are those figures that have to signify the querent and the sought thing.

For the place of the querent's significator is the first figure. Because the querent is always considered in the first house. And note that he is not the querent who asks on behalf of another, but rather he for whom he asks: he is said to be the querent, whether he is present there or not. For example: I ask on behalf of N. how it is with him; not I, but he is called the querent.

The other significator is the sign of the thing questioned, which is considered sometimes in one house, sometimes in another. For example: if one asks whether he shall make a profit, or if he shall receive money from a debtor, or if a lost thing is in the house, or if his money is arriving safe and sound, or if he shall become rich: all of this is considered in the second house.

There is another significator. And if one asks whether he is loved by his father or uncle, as well as by his father-in-law, or by any elder of his family, or if his paternal goods ought to be increased or recovered, or if it is good to found a castle or a house, or if any building project begun will come to completion, or if a thing is hidden in the place asked about, or in what part of the house, ship, land, or field the thing is hidden; and if the hidden thing ought to be found in the hoped-for place, or if a building will fall, or if the planting of trees will take effect, or if a field ought to abound in crops, or if the price of seeds will be raised or lowered in the month or year asked about; or if the land questioned is fertile, or if the thing questioned will come to a good end.

### On the Occupation of a Figure

## PDF p. 471 / ms p. 227v

Occupation is when the sign of the querent occupies the house of the thing questioned. For example: someone asked whether he could recover his house which he had lost; and there was Albus `[2, 2, 1, 2]` (<FigureGlyph dots={[2,2,1,2]} name="Albus" size={1.15} />) in the first house, and it moved into the fourth: it occupied the place of the thing questioned. And note that no method in this science is better than this. If the first sign is fortunate, then there is no doubt about acquiring the thing questioned, if God wills.

### On the Conjunction of Figures

Coniunctio is when one significator moves into conjunction with the other significator. For example: someone asked whether he could find his servant who had fled; and there was Acquisitio variant `[2, 1, 2, 1]` (<FigureGlyph dots={[2,1,2,1]} name="Acquisitio" size={1.15} />) in the first house, and it moved into the fifth: namely, into conjunction with the sixth house, which was the significatrix of that servant. Whence he judges that he ought to find the servant, because of the signification of that conjunction.

## PDF p. 472 / ms p. 228r

And note that when the first significator moves into conjunction with the other significator, it always signifies that the querent will acquire the thing questioned by his own effort and labor. And when the figure of the thing questioned goes into conjunction with the querent, then it signifies that the querent will have the thing questioned without his own effort and labor.

### On the Mutation of Significators

Mutation is when both significators move outside their own places, making a conjunction between themselves. For example: someone asked whether he could have the woman he loved; and there was Coniunctio `[2, 1, 1, 2]` (<FigureGlyph dots={[2,1,1,2]} name="Conjunctio" size={1.15} />) in the first house, and Puella `[1, 2, 1, 1]` (<FigureGlyph dots={[1,2,1,1]} name="Puella" size={1.15} />) in the seventh, the significatrix of the woman. Therefore, that mutation was made: Coniunctio `[2, 1, 1, 2]` (<FigureGlyph dots={[2,1,1,2]} name="Conjunctio" size={1.15} />) in the fourth house and Puella `[1, 2, 1, 1]` (<FigureGlyph dots={[1,2,1,1]} name="Puella" size={1.15} />) in the third house. And so, in the conjunction of the significators outside their own places, it signifies that the querent will be joined to the woman sought, but not in their own places—that is, not in the place of the querent, nor in the house of the woman.

## PDF p. 473 / ms p. 228v

But since the said conjunction was made on the part of the querent, it denotes that the querent will have the woman sought because of his own house.

### On the Translation of Figures

Translation is when a figure carries the disposition from one significator to another. For example: someone asked whether he could contract a marriage through his go-between (translator); and such a question fell out: there was Acquisitio variant `[2, 1, 2, 1]` (<FigureGlyph dots={[2,1,2,1]} name="Acquisitio" size={1.15} />) in the house of the querent and the 3-line scribal error `[2, 2, 2]` (<DotPatternGlyph dots={[2,2,2]} name="three-line scribal pattern" size={1.15} />) in his conjunction, making a good translation to the seventh, which joined it to him in the eighth house, making the disposition of marriage; and because the ascendant was Acquisitio and fortunate, and because the conjunction Coniunctio `[2, 1, 1, 2]` (<FigureGlyph dots={[2,1,1,2]} name="Conjunctio" size={1.15} />) was procreated from the first and seventh, and because the aspect of the fifth house strengthened the ascendant: nevertheless, because of the multiple succession of the same, he contracted marriage easily.

### On the Goodness and Malice of the Judge and Witnesses

## PDF p. 474 / ms p. 229r

The goodness or malice of the Judge and the Witnesses is considered in this way, which is manifest through experience. Because the 15th figure [the Judge] is closer to the artist than the rest, whence it is fully attributed to the Moon, which is closer to the Earth than the other planets. And because, just as the Moon in one lunation by its very swift course passes through all the signs and visits all the planets, separating from one and joining to another; in the same way, this 15th figure arises from the procreation and generation of all the superior figures. But because in number we have the 13th before the 14th [the First and Second Witnesses], the 13th figure is attributed to that planet from which the Moon separates, and the 14th to that which it joins. Therefore, if the 13th is good and the 14th is evil, it signifies that the thing questioned tends toward the worse. And if the 13th is evil and the 14th is good, it tends toward the better—provided indeed that the 15th favors its testimonies. The goodness or malice of the Judge does not differ from the fourth house, because both of them signify the end of the thing questioned. Whence, if both are good, there is no doubt of an excellent end of the thing questioned, if God wills.

## PDF p. 475 / ms p. 229v

And on the contrary: if one is good and the other evil, you shall judge a mediocre end from this.

### On the 16th Figure

The sixteenth figure indeed is procreated from the first and the 15th, whose goodness and malice is to be considered: if it should be good, and is found in some part of the question, it signifies that after the acquisition of the thing questioned, [some good] will pertain to him from some honor. And that good will arise from the nature of that house in which the 16th is found. If it should be evil, the whole is the contrary.

### On the Swift and Slow End of a Question

Whether the arrival or transaction of the desired thing will be swifter or slower is considered in this way. Count the points of all the figures of the said number 16. Then, if the number of points is 96 (since all figures [normally] consist of 96 points [combined]), it appears that it will be swifter, neither a slow arrival nor [long] sought. If indeed it is more than 96, it will be slow,

## PDF p. 476 / ms p. 230r

and the more points there are, the slower. And if the said points are fewer than 96, the thing questioned reaches its end so much more swiftly. And the more they exceed 96, the more slowly the end comes.

### To Know the Truth of a Question

If you wish to know [the truth of] any question, take the first figure, which is the house of life, and the 15th, which is the Judge, and make one figure from them; and take the second figure, which is the house of money (bizants), and the eighth, which is the house of death, and make another figure. And you will have two figures; take these two and make one figure from them, and take the first sign in the constellation; and as it is there, so declare. And as it says below. And if there should be two or more there, take the later one, and declare according to it; and you shall judge those things which you found in the constellation, even unto the cause about which you wished to know, and you shall see it thus. And if it is not in the twelve [houses], return to the beginning and judge according to the constellation in nature.

### To Know the Intention of the Querent

In a figure of Geomancy, to know the intention of the querent,

And where the number ends, there will be the figure which shall signify the intention of the querent. And if 12 remains, the 12th figure will be the significatrix.

### Method of Knowing the Signifying Figure

Take the points of the first and second figures, and multiply them by the points of the 9th, and from that which arises cast out 7 as many times as you can; then count from the first, giving one to each, and where the number falls, there will be the figure signifying the intention; and whether it will signify what is to come, whether good or evil, or if not. To know what is to come, take that which remains, or 8. If nothing remains, count from the first and note the figure in which the number ends, and similarly count with the same number from the 15th,

### To Make the Judge [Super-Judge]

For this purpose, so that you may know what is the sign of good or evil, extract the 16th figure, which is from the 1st and the 15th, and make one figure from it and the 13th, and another figure from that one and the 14th, and from these two figures which result, make one figure, and this will be the Judge [i.e., the Reconciler/Super-Judge].

### On the Times of the Figures

The first four [houses] are of the present. The second four are of the past, and the four which are under them [9 to 12] are of the future. And thus the 13th is of the querent, and the 14th is of that about which it is asked, and the fifteenth is of the Judgment.

### On Illness: Whence It Arises

In illness, the 15th is considered, and if it should be evil, it is considered from whence that evil arises; for if it is from the first four figures, it will be of the present; if from the second four, it will be of the past; if from the other four, it will be of the future. And if it should be from only one [group], he will be freed; and if from two, he will suffer more, but will be freed; and if from all three, he will not escape; and if the figure Populus appears twice, he will die without doubt, and the judgment will be according to the signification of the figures, because if the signification is from the first [four] only, the present evil arises. If from the second [four], it will be past. If from the other four which are under them, future. And the sick person will always be freed when the 15th is good.

### On Forming the Signifying Figure

Make one figure from the first and the 15th, and this is the 16th. Afterward, place the head of the first prior, and the head of the 15th second in the order of the figure, and third the head of the 16th under them. Finally, make a line by punctating, and make from it the completion of the figure with three heads, just as in the first ones. Afterward, make one figure from it and the first figure, and another from it and the 15th; then make one from these two which result, and consider its place in the chart, and it will signify according to its place what is to come. And if it should not be in the chart, consider it by itself, because the signification will be according to it.

### On the Properties of the Figures

<FigureGlyph dots={[2,1,2,1]} name="Acquisitio" size={2.2} />

**Acquisitio (Gain)** is contained in Aries: it is aerial, masculine, oriental and fortunate, sanguine; it has of the members of the body the head and face, of colors white and yellow (saffron), of tastes sweet, of places beautiful [pleasant areas]. For it is a good and fortunate figure, entering, and demonstrates gain suddenly and profit; and from it one should judge well, especially in matters of acquisition, and it signifies a person of moderate height with a brown face, faithful, and with a long face.

## PDF p. 488 / ms p. 236r

<FigureGlyph dots={[1,2,1,2]} name="Amissio" size={2.2} />

**Amissio (Loss)** is contained in Scorpio, and is an unfortunate figure, southern, masculine, fiery, choleric; it has of the members of the body the genitals, of colors mixed, of tastes bitter, of places uninhabited [desolated], and signifies the diminution of substance; and a person of short height, of saffron color, with a large head and a round face, who delights much in eating and in things of Venus (sensual pleasures), and it signifies deceit and a corrupted woman.

<FigureGlyph dots={[2,2,1,1]} name="Fortuna Major" size={2.2} />

**Fortuna maior (Greater Fortune)** is in Aquarius, and is a fortunate, feminine, entering figure, cold and dry, earthy, melancholic, western; it has of tastes sweet, of colors pink and white, of places the palaces of kings; and it signifies a person of average height, of good will and sweet aspect, who is very bashful, with varied [flecked] eyes, a broad chest, a round face, rather slender shoulders; and it is an excellent figure, fortunate in all things: for it gives tribute in peace. For the sick, however, in the 6th house it is evil, and in the 7th it signifies death.

## PDF p. 489 / ms p. 236v

<FigureGlyph dots={[1,1,2,2]} name="Fortuna Minor" size={2.2} />

**Fortuna minor (Lesser Fortune)** is contained in Taurus, and is a moderately fortunate figure, fiery, hot and dry, choleric, masculine, southern; it has of tastes bitter, of colors white; and it signifies a person of average height, with a round face, a large nose, eyes of scarlet color (coccineum), that is, between black and varied [flecked], and it signifies a bold, proud, and noble person, and is best in lawsuits and in battle.

<FigureGlyph dots={[1,1,1,1]} name="Via" size={2.2} />

**Via (The Way)** is contained in Leo, it is also watery, cold and wet, phlegmatic, northern, feminine, nocturnal; it has of tastes sweet mixed with salty, of colors green, and signifies a tall, slender person, with a long and beautiful face, who has sparse teeth, who walks willingly. It is a double-bodied figure, and signifies departure (exit) and the way.

<FigureGlyph dots={[2,2,2,2]} name="Populus" size={2.2} />

**Populus (The People)** is in Capricorn, and is a mixed figure, watery, cold and wet, northern, phlegmatic; it has of tastes salty with sweet, of colors white with red, and signifies a person of complete [full] height, with a round face, a large body, with a mark on the face, a changeable person of many thoughts and many words, and it signifies a corrupted woman, and is a convertible figure, and always signifies a multitude.

## PDF p. 490 / ms p. 237r

<FigureGlyph dots={[2,1,1,1]} name="Caput Draconis" size={2.2} />

**Caput Draconis (The Dragon's Head)** is contained in Virgo, and is a fortunate, cold, dry, melancholic, earthy, feminine, western figure; it has of colors saffron [yellow], of tastes sour (acidic), and signifies a person of average height with a round face, a large nose, a large mouth, large teeth, much hair, and it signifies a chaste woman or a virgin, and the acquisition of all things.

<FigureGlyph dots={[1,1,1,2]} name="Cauda Draconis" size={2.2} />

**Cauda Draconis (The Dragon's Tail)** is in Sagittarius, it is a fiery figure, hot and dry, masculine, choleric, southern, unfortunate; it has of tastes harsh and cruel, of colors pink with saffron, and signifies a person of average height, with a round face, a small bald head; it signifies a corrupted woman of the same form, and loss in all things. rt.

## PDF p. 491 / ms p. 237v

**Albus (White)** is in Cancer, it is a fortunate figure, cold and wet, watery, phlegmatic, northern, feminine; it has of tastes sweet, of colors white and green, and signifies a white (fair) person of average height, kind, with a large head and envious eyes, whose forehead often sweats, and signifies the retention of income or the increase of those things which are desired. For the sick, in the 6th, 7th, 8th, and 12th houses it is evil, because it signifies tears and lamentation; and it signifies a kind and chaste woman, having a changeable heart, and it signifies abundance of milk in questions of journeys.
