When in the sixt house ye finde this figure called Fortuna minor, it signifieth that the servants and subjectes be true unto their maister: but the servants shall be sicke in their service: it is ill to buy beastes, for there will be but small profite by them: the witnesses be not false, but wil by their disposition use some spitefulnes. For the Physitian, and to take that which he ordeyneth it is good, so that the fift and seventh house doe consent: the beast lost shall never be found: it is good for whores and baudes, but they shall be in daunger to be destroyed by their suddennesse and feare, that they shall not have the money which was to the[m] promised. In all other thinges this figure is meane.
