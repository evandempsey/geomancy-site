Finding in this house this figure Tristitia, it is an ill time and unlucky to attaine unto the honor pretended : the King is in daunger to lose his Realme and Dominion : it is ill for the Phisitian, or to take phisicke : the thing lost will never be had againe : it is ill for the mother and signifyeth that she is miscontented : the King or Lorde love not the person: if the king be sicke he shall die : he that pretendeth shall not be chosen Emperour or King, unlesse it be by treason or suborning of some folks: the man shall not be driven from the Court, but shall remaine long in perplexitie and disquietnesse : the King shall be betrayed: the voyage shall be long and slowe : the ayre shall be tenebrous and darke. And to be briefe, in any question which ye may propound in this house, this figure is ill, saving to till landes, buy heritages, fortifie and keepe townes, whereunto it is good, and also for treasure hid in the earth.
