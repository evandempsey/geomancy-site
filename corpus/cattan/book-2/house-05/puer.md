Finding this Figure called Puer in the fift house, it signifyeth the childe to bee of ill conversation, and disobedient unto father and mother: for governement of eating and drincking it is ill, the promise shall not be performed, the messenger will not stay long before he come and bring letters, which make mention of warre: it is not good for a friend, nor for pastime, but in seats of warre, the dwelling in the house is not good: it is worth nothing to kisse, coll, or such like dalliance, but in the venereall actes: the woman with childe shall have a sonne, the place beseeged shall not be taken, but defend it selfe valiantly: there bee many theeves lying in waite by the way, the sonne is not dead, but hurte, the childe is a bastard, ill to buy and put on newe clothes or other things unlesse it be harnes and armour, for the which it is good: the yeare shall be indifferent good, saving that there shall be some haile: the Bookes speake of warre, because this is a figure of ♂ which is lorde of warre, it signifieth likewise fewe children, and they shall be all men of warre, and pillers of poore men and laborers of the field.
