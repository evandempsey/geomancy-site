Finding this fygure Carcer in the seaventh house, the enemy is strong and boisterous, and is secret, and dissembleth in his doings, so that ye can know nothing of him: the querant shall have good successe in his sute: the theefe hath stolen much secretly, the fugitive will never come againe: it is ill for marriage, for there will be some lightnes in the woman, the woman is poore, the woman loveth not her husband: the gamster shall lose in the beginning, but winne in the ende: the man shall be taken in battell and have no victory: the fellow is ill and loveth not his wife, the maide is a virgine: in all the demaundes which ye can demaunde in this house this fygure is ill, except it be to take a prisoner.
