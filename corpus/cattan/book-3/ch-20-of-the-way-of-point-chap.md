### Of the way of point. Chap. 20.



<div class="source-locator source-locator--block">p. 194</div>

The Geomanciens have many Rules to judge their figures aright, and amongest others, that which they doe call the way of point, as a thing much necessarie and profitable in this Arte. After therefore that the figure is made with the witnesses and the Judge, you shall take the first point of that Judge, be it even or odde: and afterwardes the points of the right witnes, if it be like unto the said Judge, if not, then take the points of the left Judge, if it be like unto the Judge. But if the two witnesses have not points above like to the said Judge, then is there no way of point in y[e] figure: but if it happen that one of the witnesses be like to the Judge, and that in the upper parte the points be even or uneven, as the said judge is, yee shal take the 9. or 10. fygure, whether of those two be like to the Judge. And

<div class="source-locator source-locator--block">p. 195</div>

if it be the 9. you shall also have regard to the first & second: if the point on high be like unto that of the Judge, witnesses and ninth, you shall determine the question for the which the figure hath beene made. By the first house, if the way of point goe into the second, ye shall judge according to the second, & then if it be good ye shal judge good, and if it be ill you shal judge ill. But if the way of point goe into the tenth, you must have respect unto the third and fourth, to whether of them that is like in the points on high of the Judge and witnesses. And so shall you judge all the figure, according unto that which is like unto the Judge. And if the left witnes be like unto the Judge, you shall take the eleventh or twelfth, whether of them is like unto the Judge: and then if it be the eleventh, ye shall have respect unto the fift and sixt, and according unto that of the two shall be like on high in points unto the judge, witnesses and eleventh house you shall judge all the question propounded. And if it be good, ye shall judge it to good, and if it be evill, ye shall judge it to evill. If the twelfth have like points on high unto the witnesses and the judges, yee shall have respect unto the seaventh and eight, and according to that which of them twaine shall be like, you shall judge (as before is saide) that is, to good if it be good, and unto ill if it be ill. For ye must understand that the way of point intendeth properly unto one point, for when they be two it is not so perfite as when they be found in one: and behold this example.

<div class="source-locator source-locator--block">p. 196</div>

A figure and an example of the way of point.

[chart: a complete scheme pricked in star-points within a ruled frame — eight figures in the first row, four in the second, the two witnesses in the third, and the Judge beneath]

A Friend of mine desired me to make a figure for to knowe, whether that a servant of his had robbed him or not: and by chance I made him this figure which ye here see. And bicause that therein the first house is not very good, and because that in the house wherein the demaunds of servants be made, which is the sixt, I found an ill fygure called Cauda draconis, the which doth signifie robbery, I judged that the servant was a theefe. And bicause the seventh which is an ill figure signifies the same: and because the second which signifieth the substance of the master doth goe into that which containeth the demaundes which be upon substance: I judged that it was a signe that the Maister had beene robbed by the servant before his saide Maister did knowe thereof, and had stolen much more then was missed. And because that the regard Trine and Sextile be figures of ☿ and ☉, I say that the servant should be taken in doing the facte, which thing the fourth doth also signifie the same. And because

<div class="source-locator source-locator--block">p. 197</div>

the way of point goeth into one of the figures of ☉, by that it is manifest that the saide servant shall be discovered by means of the goodes which he hath given unto his kinsfolkes. This is the way to judge a figure by the way of point.
