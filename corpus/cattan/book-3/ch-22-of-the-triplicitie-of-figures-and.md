### Of the Triplicitie of figures, and of their face. Chap. 22.



<div class="source-locator source-locator--block">p. 199</div>

Because that a figure can not well be judged if that besides all these thinges aforesaide, a partie understand not the exaltation and the fall of the fygures, as I have alreadie declared in my first Booke, in ech Chapter where I have treated thereof, and minde here in my third Booke to doe the like of the saide figures, and of their Triplicitie and face: the which because it can not as well be done as that of the Planets, I will make no long discourse thereof, but this yee must onely understand, that as often as yee finde three fygures all of one Element, and of one qualitie, that is called a Triplicitie, and three Planets have dominion over them, one by the day and an other by the night, as ye may here see by example, where Aquisitio and Latitia, and that which commeth of them, which is Puer, do make one Triplicitie. Over the first whereof, ♃ governeth by day, and ♂ by night: and upon the other after ♂ is Lord by day, and ♃ by night, and over the third ♃ by day, and ♂ by night.

There is an other manner of Triplicitie, which is more certaine then this which I have spoken of, and that is, yee must devide all the figures into foure partes as yee see here above, and of ech part take three fygures, all of one Element, and all of one qualitie, which three, make the Triplicitie that we speake of. Unto the which Triplicitie one Planet governeth by day, and an other by night: as if Fortuna major be found in the first house, Cauda draconis in the second, and Albus in the third: that is a triplicitie: upon which ♀ governeth by day, and ♂ by night: and so of the other Planets. And note that to judge a fygure well, above all thinges you must have a regarde unto the triplicitie, for because it is verie necessarie for a judgement. And that because if that the three parties of the fygures be of the ayre, you shall judge according to the signifycation of the fygures of the

<div class="source-locator source-locator--block">p. 201</div>

Aire, which be in triplicitie. So that if three partes of the figure be of one Element, and of one qualitie, you shall judge the fygure according to the condition of the triplicitie. And if the fygure be halfe of one triplicitie, and halfe of an other, you shall judge according to that part which is the stronger, and according to the nature and condition of the Elements and Planets, which shall be the Governours of that Triplicitie: and here is an example.
