### To knowe what an other thinketh. Chap. 19.



<div class="source-locator source-locator--block">p. 193</div>

Following the Rules here before placed, to knowe to what end a Figure hath beene made, I have thought it good by the way of pleasure and recreation to place other Rules to knowe the thought of any person, not to the intent that any man give any credit or trust to it more then to the rest of the Arte, which was for none other purpose invented (as I have many times protested and said) but for the pastime and pleasure of the wittie heads and inventive. If therfore yee would know the thought of any person, you must make a fygure, and then judge according to the fygures which come thereof: and beholde the Rules like unto them which goe before.
