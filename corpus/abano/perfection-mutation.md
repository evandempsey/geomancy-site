### On the Mutation of Significators

Mutation is when both significators move outside their own places, making a conjunction between themselves. For example: someone asked whether he could have the woman he loved; and there was Coniunctio in the first house, and Puella in the seventh, the significatrix of the woman. Therefore, that mutation was made: Coniunctio in the fourth house and Puella in the third house. And so, in the conjunction of the significators outside their own places, it signifies that the querent will be joined to the woman sought, but not in their own places—that is, not in the place of the querent, nor in the house of the woman.

But since the said conjunction was made on the part of the querent, it denotes that the querent will have the woman sought because of his own house.
