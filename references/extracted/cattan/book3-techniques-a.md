A briefe deduction of the accord and signification which the sixteene figures have by the twelve houses.

CHAP. 1.

{margin: 1. 2, dot-figure of Aquisitio} IF this figure named *Aquisitio*, do in mounting growe from the first house into the seconde, that is to say, that he be in the second or be like unto the first where *Aquisitio* is, it signifyeth to gaine gownes, and clothing apparell, to get honour, and to make gaine and profit in all acts of honour and vertue, and with the like people.

{margin: 1. 3} If that from the first she goe into the third, it signifyeth to have honour, joy, and profite of the kinsefolks and friends.

{margin: 1. 4} If that shee goe from the first to the fourth, it signifyeth profite by the father, and a good and joyfull end of his affaires.

{margin: 1. 5} If that shee goe from the first to the fyft, it signifyeth joy of children, that is to say, they shall be good, and of honour, and to have joy and pleasure, to eate and drinke, and to bee well clothed.

{margin: 1. 6} If that she goe from the first to the sixt, it signifyeth profite and good lucke for servants, and to buy small cattell.

{margin: 1. 7} If she goe into the seventh, it signifyeth to make a contract of enemies, to make marriages, and to get honour and the love of persons, and for all demaundes it is good.

{margin: 1. 8} If she goe from the first into the eight, it signifyeth death for the thing demaunded, or for the demaunder and great profite in succession and heritages : it is also good for thinges of magicke.

{margin: 1. 9} If she goe from the first to the ninth, it signifyeth to have profite and gaine by voyages, and in things of the Church, as in benefices or offices : it is also good for the profite of the children that yee would put to schole, for they shall have promotions Ecclesiasticall, and they shall be men of knowledge.

{margin: 1. 10} If she passe from the fyrst to the tenth house, it signifyeth amitie, familiaritie, acquaintance and profite with kings, princes, and great lordes: likewise to receive honour and dignitie by the mother : and also profite in succession and heritages. And to be briefe, it is good in all things.

{margin: 1. 11} If she goe from the fyrst to the eleventh, it signifyeth to have honour and profite by his friendes, and a good hope of the question demaunded.

{margin: 1. 12} If she passe from the fyrst to the twelfth, it signifyeth imprisonment, losse of beastes and to be overcome by the enemie: and to be briefe, it is ill in all thinges.

{margin: 1. 2, dot-figure of Amissio} If you fynde this fygure *Amissio* in the fyrst house, and from thence be found in the seconde house, which we call the going from one house unto an other, it signifyeth losse of goods, and to fall into debates, quarrelles and contentions and sute for goods.

{margin: 1. 3} If she passe into the third house, it signifyeth anger and contentions betweene kinsfolks and allyes, it is also ill for small and short journeyes.

{margin: 1. 4} If she goe into the fourth house, it signifyeth anger betweene the children and the father, and betweene the father and the children, and by that meanes losse of heritages, and the beginning and sute in lawe the one against the other : and this copulation and assembly of the fygure is very ill but for lecherie.

{margin: 1. 5} If she passe into the fift house, the woman with childe shall have unseasonable deliverance : it is also ill to eate and drinke, and signifyeth anger about victuals.

{margin: 1. 6} If she passe into the sixt it signifyeth anger against servants, and losse of them and of small cattell.

{margin: 1. 7} If she passe thence into the seventh, it signifyeth strifes, quarels and debates with friendes : it is also ill for marriages, and signifyeth to have displeasure with his wife, and to lose the sute.

{margin: 1. 8} If she goe into the eight, it signifyeth mortalitie by hoat fevers, and alteration of the understanding, losse of goods of the women, and of the enemie : and in all the demaundes it is ill.

{margin: 1. 9} If she passe into the ninth, it signifyeth losse of substance, and to be robbed by the way : it is ill for substance of the Church, and for men learned.

{margin: 1. 10} If she goe into the tenth, it signifyeth anger and miscontentment of kings, princes and lordes, and of the sea, and it is ill in all the demaundes of the tenth house.

{margin: 1. 11} If shee passe into the eleventh, it signifyeth anger against his friend, and to have an ill issue of the thing pretented or hoped to have.

{margin: 1. 12} If she goe into the twelfth, it signifyeth losse of cattell, and imprisonment. And to be short, it is ill in all things.

{margin: 1. 2, dot-figure of Fortuna maior} If this figure *Fortuna maior* be in the first house, and thence leape into the second, it signifyeth a great gaine in goods, moveables, as gold, silver, and rich utensilles.

{margin: 1. 3} If she passe into the third house, it signifyeth to have much substance by the meanes of the kindred : it is also good for small and short voyages.

{margin: 1. 4} If it passe into the fourth, it signifyeth the father to be of good and godly disposition: it is also good to buy heritages and possessions, and a good issue in all things.

{margin: 1. 5} If shee goe into the fift, it signifyeth joy, pleasure, to be in good order, well appointed and clothed, to eate and drinke well, and to have fewe children, and they shall be of red colour.

{margin: 1. 6} If it passe into the sixt, it signifyeth fidelitie in servantes, and that the sicke person shall amende: it is also good for small beastes.

{margin: 1. 7} If shee passe into the seventh it is good for marriage, and signifyeth that the enemie will make peace : for the acquaintance of a Ladie it is not good, because their secretes shall bee disclosed.

{margin: 1. 8} If it goe into the eight, it signifyeth death : it is ill in all things but to have the goods of a woman.

{margin: 1. 9} If shee passe into the ninth, it signifyeth to contract an amitie with Church men, and men learned, to make a voyage it is good.

{margin: 1. 10} If it goe into the tenth, it signifyeth to have dignitie with kings and princes, and with the mother, and to have victorie over his enemies.

{margin: 1. 11} If shee passe into the eleventh it signifyeth a good issue upon a good hope, good friendes and succorable.

{margin: 1. 12} If it goe into the twelfth it signifyeth imprisonment of the Prince, and that his enemies shall over passe him in might and power.

{margin: 1. 2, dot-figure of Fortuna minor} If this figure *Fortuna minor*, be in the first house, & thence goe into the second, which we call going from one house to an other, it signifyeth a meane in gaine, and he which medleth with red things shall quickly lose thereby.

{margin: 1. 3} If she passe into the third, it signifyeth joy of the kinsfolkes, it is also good for a short voyage, and signifyeth that it shall bee quickly ended, it is also good for friendes.

{margin: 1. 4} If it passe into the fourth, it signifyeth that the father is angry or sicke, it also signifyeth the losse of the sute.

{margin: 1. 5} If she come into the fift, it signifyeth to have many children which shall be red of colour, also great joy and pleasure, to eate and drinke, and to be costly and well apparelled.

{margin: 1. 6} And if she go into the sixt, it signifyeth good to buy small cattle, it is indifferent for servants, and signifyeth that they shall be wittie and wise.

{margin: 1. 7} If it passe into the seventh, it signifyeth marriage, and that it shall be with strife and contention, the enemie is strong and mightie, the woman is wise, it is ill in case of love, for it shall be discovered.

{margin: 1. 8} If shee goe into the eight, it signifyeth death, and also is ill in all things of esprite, as Magicke and invocations.

{margin: 1. 9} If it passe into the ninth, it signifyeth long voyages with all prosperitie and good lucke, and the amitie of men of the Church.

{margin: 1. 10} If shee goe into the tenth, it is good and prosperous going to the service of a king, Prince or great Lord, the shippe on the Sea shall come safe and sounde, and the owner thereof shall shortly have newes.

{margin: 1. 11} If it passe into the eleventh, it signifieth good and trustie friendes, and a good end of the thing wherein hope is put.

{margin: 1. 12} If shee goe into the twelfth, it is ill in all things but to buy horses.

{margin: dot-figure of Rubeus} If you finde *Rubeus* in the first house, I will say no other thing but that which before I have tolde you, that is to say, that following the opinion of all the Doctors in this science, be they Caldeans, Indians, Hebrewes, Arabyes, Egyptians or Persians, when this figure is founde in this place, it ought not to be judged, the which thing I have alwayes founde true by long experience, wherefore at this time I will say no other thing : but that she is ill in all the houses but in the sixt.

{margin: 1. 2, dot-figure of Albus} If you finde this fygure *Albus* in the fyrst house, and be found againe in the second house, it signifyeth gaine and profite in white things, writings or letters, or bookes.

{margin: 1. 3} If she passe into the third, it signifyeth good time for friendes and kinsfolkes, and that letters shall come from neare at hand.

{margin: 1. 4} If it goe into the fourth, it signifyeth to winne his sute, it is also good in the demaundes which may be made upon the father, signifying as well in them as in all others a good issue and ende.

{margin: 1. 5} If she passe into the fift, it signifyeth to have many children, and to delight in white clothes, to eate and drinke, and to company often times with learned men.

{margin: 1. 6} If it passe into the sixt the servants be good and trustie.

{margin: 1. 7} If she goe into the seventh the marriage lately begunne shall take effect to the great contentment and profite of the parties, and the enemies shall demaund peace.

{margin: 1. 8} And if it passe into the eight, it signifyeth death by a hoat disease of rumes and cathers, it is also good for magicke.

{margin: 1. 9} If she goe into the ninth, it signifyeth that the long journey shalbe good and profitable, and that the letters which come from a farre off bring good newes, it is also good for Doctors & churchmen, and to make amitie and acquaintance with them.

{margin: 1. 10} If it passe into the tenth it is good to goe to Kings, Princes and Lordes, and to goe to visite his mother.

{margin: 1. 11} If shee goe into the eleventh it bringeth good lucke, and also for letters which shall come on their part.

{margin: 1. 12} If it passe into the twelfth, it is good to buye frames, also in this place it is a token to be held prisoner.

{margin: 1. 2, dot-figure of Caput draconis} If this fygure *Caput draconis* be in the first house, & thence ascende into the seconde, it signifyeth gayne and profite in all things.

{margin: 1. 3} If it passe into the third, it signifyeth the kinsfolkes to be of good amitie, it is also good for short voyages, but that there shall be some slownesse therein.

{margin: 1. 4} If she goe into the fourth it signifyeth good for the father, and to succeede in his heritage.

{margin: 1. 5} If it passe into the fift it sygnifyeth many children which shal be wise and wittie, it sheweth also a heartinesse to eate and drinke.

{margin: 1. 6} If she goe into the sixt it signifyeth gaine and profite in buying of cattell, and that the servants be good and loyall.

{margin: 1. 7} If it passe into the seventh it is a good time for marriage and for reconciliation of enemyes, and that the person is of great amitie.

{margin: 1. 8} If shee goe into the eight it signifyeth death without remedie, and sometime in this place it signifyeth to winne inheritaunces.

{margin: 1. 9} If it passe into the ninth, it signifyeth gaine and profite by a long voyage by men of the Church.

{margin: 1. 10} If shee goe into the tenth it is good to goe to Kings, Princes and to the mother.

{margin: 1. 11} If it passe into the eleventh it signifyeth to have good friends, and that the hope shall not be in vaine : and also the thing that is demaunded shall come to a good effect.

{margin: 1. 12} If she goe into the twelfth, it sygnifyeth to be made prisoner, and therein to have much vexation and torment, and in all the demaundes she is ill, but for to buy horses.

{margin: dot-figure of Cauda draconis} If this figure *Cauda draconis* be found in the first house, then the figure should not be judged, but it must be broken and an other made one houre after that : but if from the seconde house she goe into any of the other ye may there judge it, saving for the fourth, where there is no certaine judgement to be given, for the malice of the sayde fygure and therefore at this time I will say no more.

{margin: 1. 2, dot-figure of Læticia} Finding this fygure *Læticia* in the first house, and thence go into the seconde, it signifyeth a mediocritie of gaine by white thinges, and in thinges of the Church.

{margin: 1. 3} If it passe into the third, it signifyeth the kinsfolkes to be merry and in good disposition and in health of body, it is also good for small and short voyages.

{margin: 1. 4} If she goe into the fourth, the end of all thinges shall be joyfull, but the promise false.

{margin: 1. 5} If it passe into the fift, it is good for children, and to eate and drinke, and to sing musicke, and signifyeth a great lightsomnesse of heart.

{margin: 1. 6} If she goe into the sixt, it is good for servauntes, signifying that they shall be profitable to their masters: in all the other houses this fygure is good, but in the eight and twelfth, for in the eight she signifyeth death, and in the twelfth losse of cattell. And for because that according to the signification of ech house I have herebefore sufficiently written : you may by the same easely judge ech question that is demaunded, I will not hold you long in the other fygures which followe in touching their mutations and concordances, but onely passe over them generally.

{margin: dot-figure of Tristitia} This fygure *Tristitia* found in the first, and thence goe into the second, it signifyeth small profite but in things of the earth, as vines, medowes, woodes and lands earrable, in all the other houses this figure is ill, but in the fourth and eight where both in the one and the other, it is good to buy heritages, & because I have largely declared here before, I will nowe say nothing more at this time.

{margin: dot-figure of Puella} If this fygure *Puella* goe from the first house to the second, it signifyeth gaine by women, and by white things, in all the other houses where this figure passeth shee is good, especially in the house where she signifyeth joyfulnesse : but in the eight she signifyeth death, and in the twelfth imprisonment.

{margin: dot-figure of Puer} If this figure *Puer*, passeth from the first to the second, it signifyeth small gaine but in things of warre, in all other houses this figure is meane, but in the eight and twelfth, she is ill.

{margin: dot-figure of Conjunctio} This figure *Conjunctio* going from the first to the second, it signifyeth gaine by bookes and writings, in all other houses this figure is indifferent, but in the seventh where it is good for marriage, and in the ninth to put schollers to studie, and in the eight it is ill, for it alwayes signifyeth death, and in the twelfth it is neither good nor badde.

{margin: dot-figure of Carcer} This figure *Carcer* passing from the first into the second it is gainefull to buy landes earrable, in all the other houses where ye finde her, ye shall judge her as ye doe the others, that is to say, according to the signification of the house, where shee is, and therefore at this time I will speake no more thereof.

{margin: dot-figure of Populus} This figure *Populus* passing fro[m] the first house to the second, it signifyeth gaine and profite in white things, and in landes which lye by the water, and if she passe into the third, it is good to make voyage by water, it signifyeth death in the eight and in the ninth, and in the twelfth to be taken prisoner. In all the other houses judge according to their significations.

{margin: dot-figure of Via} This figure *Via* going from the first to the second, it signifyeth small gaine, if it passe into the third it is good for short voyages : in all the other houses this figure is ill, but in the ninth and tenth, where she is very good, in the eight shee signifyeth death, and prisonment in the twelfth.

When so ever ye finde the first houses to passe one into an other, you shall judge according to the signification of the house where they passe, as I have in the beginning tolde you at large, and now in the end more briefely, the which things to the ende ye may the better understande them, I will set you an example of a figure which my Lord of Tays commaunded me to make, to knowe whether the French king Francis the first of that name, and the Emperour Charles of Austriche, the fift of that name, should speake together: which figure being made, and by fortune *Aquisitio* being in the first house, went straight into the seventh, which is the house of kings and Emperours which was the cause that I straight way judged that the Emperour should speake with the king, and so likewise shall yee judge of the second if it passe into the third, to knowe (as by way of example) if the demaund be made for substance it signifyeth that the substance shall come into the handes of the kinsfolkes of him which made the question according to the content of the demaund, and the signifycation of the house where the sayde figure is : if the second passe into the third, fift or sixt, or into any of the others following, you shall also judge according to the signifycation of the house where it is : the like shall ye do by the third if it passe into the fourth, or into any of the other, unto the twelfth, so shall ye doe by the fourth if she passe into the fift, or into any of the other houses following: and so al the others judging as is aforesaide, according to the signifycations of the houses where they goe, and according to the good or ill of the sayde figures. And ye must note that they never passe but one time to stay the judgement, although that a figure formed have many of one sorte, and all a like, whereof I will speake more at large hereafter in declaring the example that I will set.

Of the good or ill house, and which they be, where the figures be in their places. Chap. 2.

THe good houses, to be briefe, are the first, fift, tenth and eleventh : the meane houses be the second, third, fourth and ninth : the evill houses be the sixt, seventh, eight and twelfth houses.

The houses wherein the figures be found to be good.

*Aquisitio* is good for profite, and amongst al other figures it is good in the first, second, and tenth house.

*Amissio* is good for losse of substance, and therefore is good in the eight house, and very ill in the second.

*Fortuna maior* is good for gaine in things where a person hath hope to winne, and therefore it is very good in the fift, sixt, ninth and eleventh houses.

*Fortuna minor* is good in any affaire, wherein a person would goe quickely, and is therefore very good in the second house, and ill in the eight house.

*Latitia* is good for joy, as well present as to come, and for that cause is founde good almost in all the houses, and specially in the fift, and ill in the sixt, eight and twelfth houses.

*Tristitia* is a very ill figure in all the houses, but in the eight and twelfth houses, where she is good : and meane in the first and second houses.

*Albus* is good for a man which hopeth to have gaine or profite in any thing, and also to have entrie into any place, and in this respect is found good in the first and fourth houses.

*Rubeus* is ill in all good things, and good in all ill things, and many times signifyeth death, she is never found in the first house to make a judgement as is tolde you before, she is ill in the second, fourth, seaventh and tenth houses, and almost in all the other, saving in certaine demaundes.

*Puella* is very good in all things that ye may demaunde, and especially in things of women, and she is very good in the ninth and fift houses.

*Puer* is very ill in all the questions and demaundes which may be made in all the houses, saving in the second, and sixte where he is meane.

*Carcer* is a fygure likewise ill in all the houses, and especially in the sixt, eight, seventh and twelfth houses, and signifyeth alwayes to be stayed.

*Conjunctio* is good with good, and ill with ill, and signifyeth alwayes a recoverment and restitution of thinges scattered or lost, and she is found good in the seventh, ninth and tenth houses, and ill in the eight, and signifieth death, & in the twelfth signifyeth to be kept in prison.

*Caput draconis* is good with good, and ill with ill, and is good in the seventh and second houses, and sheweth to have a good issue in the things where a man hopeth to have gaine.

*Cauda draconis* is very good with the ill, and very ill with the good, in matter of losse she is good, and to passe out of an affaire: she is found good in the fourth, sixt, ninth and twelfth houses, and ill in the second : ye must note that in the ninth she is good to learne Science, and ill to journey, signifying spoiling and robbing, she is also ill in this place for all other things.

*Populus* is sometime good and sometime bad, with good she is good, and with ill she is ill, she is good in the tenth, and ill in the eight house.

*Via* is a fygure which breaketh and spoileth al the goodnes of the others, saving in demaundes of journeys and voyages, and to goe from place to place to the which she is very good, she is good in the third, fift and seventh houses, because she signifyeth that letters shall come which shall bring good newes: in the twelfth house she is common.

Of the two witnesses. Chap. 3.

AFter that we have sufficiently treated of the 12. houses and of the fygures and of their translation and concorde, and which be good and which be bad. Now resteth it to speake of the two witnesses and of the Judge, now must you know that the two witnesses be two figures drawen of the twelve figures of the Zodiacke, whereof the one must be placed in the Figure formed on the right side, which is the thirteenth Figure, and is called the right witnes, the other must be placed on the left side of the Figure, and is the fourteenth Figure named the left witnes : the right witnes is put for the querant, and signifyeth all that which by the first Figure, and all the others which bee on the right side (that is to say the second, third, fourth, ninth and tenth) is discerned, which is the motive of the question before propounded. The left witnes containeth all that which the figures doe signifie which be on his side, that is to say, the fift, sixt, seventh, eight, eleventh and twelfth, on such wise that the right witnes signifyeth the querant, and the left witnes the thing demaunded, propounded and enquired. Besides this, the right witnes signifyeth joy and happines of the thing lately passed to the person which propounded the question, and the left witnesse signifyeth heavinesse, unquietnesse and mishap of the thing to come, and put in question : you must further note, that these two witnesses be no houses, neither naturall Figures, but bee onely accidentalles, taken from the other to give a judgement certaine on the question propounded.

Of the Judge. Chap. 4.

THe Judge or the fifteenth figure is procreated of the two witnesses to judge the ende of all the signification of the demaund, to know if it be good or bad. The which judge ought alwayes of necessitie to be even : for if it be not, the figure should be false : and so if the Judge be good, the signification of the demaund shall come to a good ende, and if he be ill, it shall come to an ill ende. If the Judge doe agree with the first, and with the other fygures which be on the right side, it signifyeth good to the querant and in the thing demaunded. And if he agree wyth those of the left hand which be called the daughters, it signifyeth to the querant an ill issue of the thing demaunded. And so must ye say and esteeme of the accorde which he hath with the right or left witnesse, as ye shall see by the example following, according to the doctours in this Arte, as well Hebrewes as Chaldeans, and other which have treated thereof.

This Table following shall serve you to knowe the Witnesses and the Judge as wel even as uneven, and the signification which they have, and also for the better playing : the judgement of eache figure and demaunde propounded.

ANd thus hath beene described unto you by this Table here before, by a discourse, the signification of the Judge, and of the witnesses even and uneven, to the ende you may the easelier give a certaine judgement of the Figures as they shall fall, having sometimes like signification and importance for sundry demaundes, and sometime unlike, as to knowe if that a person shall be of long life or shorte, if he shall have the goodes and succession of his father, if it be good to buy landes and lordships, if it be good to take a wife, and whether she shall be good or bad, and whether a woman with childe shall have a sonne or a daughter, or if a sicke person shall amend of that disease, or that he which is in prison shall quickely come forth, or if it be good to take a voyage in hand, whether a thing lost shall be founde againe. And so of all other questions and demaundes which may be named, according to the example here before set out, whereby you may by your owne selfe without any further declaration knowe the Figures which be good for one demaund, and those which holde no more on the one side then on the other. Moreover, I have here placed the figure *Populus* for a Judge, contrarie to the opinion of all the Doctors in this Science, the which say all with one accorde, that when she is found in this place, that then the figure formed is not to be made, or question propounded, is not to be judged by him, but that question must bee judged by the foure angles and other rules, which wee will hereafter shewe, by the which they may be as well and certainely judged, as by the Judge himselfe.

How many manner of waies a figure is saide to be well made and fortunate or unfortunate. Chap. 6.

YOu must knowe that a figure consisting (as we have saide before) of fifteene Figures, that is to say, foure mothers, foure daughters, foure nephews, two witnesses, and one judge, is saide to be good and well made for three reasons. The first is, when the Figure doth consist uppon many good figures. The second is, when the Figure is made of figures which have more even points then odde. The third is, when it is of more then ninetie six points, or of so many at the least.

You must further understand, that a fygure is saide to bee good or unfortunate eight manner of wayes. First, by the nature and condition of the figure. Secondly, by the propertie thereof. Thirdly, by the place wherein it is. Fourthly, because of the aspect. Fiftly, by the company. Sixtly, for the variation and changing of the figures. Seaventhly, because of their moving. Eightly, by the lawfull creation of the same fygure. She is said to be good or ill by nature, first when two figures doe agree in nature and condition, as when they be both of one Element, or both alike : the second is by the propertie of the figure, that is to say, when the figure in the question formed is founde properly in the place of the thing demaunded, and when it doth agree with the said thing demanded: the third is, because of the place, which is, when it is founde in a good or ill place. The fourth, because of the aspect, which is good when a good figure in the question formed is founde in a place of good aspect, as by example, if the first figure be *Aquisitio*, which is a good fygure, and then in the eleventh house the same fygure bee found, or else *Fortuna maior* or an other like which is a good figure, or that the figure which doth beholde the other be of the same Element, then the saide question or figure made is said to be fortunate, or if it be to the contrarie, it is saide to be ill. The fift is, because of the company, that is, when the fygure is associated of an other good fygure by the good happe, or of an other ill by an ill fortune. The sixt, because of variation, that is, when one figure is good, and another ill, the one direct, the other retrograde, as when the first is good, the second ill, or contrarie, or when the first is good, and his aspect ill, and so consequently. The seventh is for the moving, as when the first figure moveth once, twice or thrice or more times by the houses, or the second, third or fourth. The eight is by creation, that is to say, when one figure is made by his nature and custome to be made, which is by sixteene lines or pointes, which by nature is called legitimate, or that it is made by Arte, as the daughters, nephewes, witnes and judge, or by triplicitie. Wherefore it is diligently to be considered, to see if the saide fygures be engendred of others either good or ill fygures, of good fathers or of good mothers: for ye must note that the right fygure is the father, and the lefte fygure is the mother : if the fygure ingendred be like unto the father, it shall be called his sonne, and if it be like unto the mother it shall be called hir daughter. And yee must further understand, that the place sometime maketh the fygure masculine, and sometime feminine. For the first house is alwayes masculine, the second feminine, the third masculine, the fourth feminine, and so of the other houses. All the fygures of the Fire and of the Ayre be masculine, and those of the Water and of the Earth be feminine: all which things must be considered upon, in giving good judgement upon any fygure, for without these rules all the judgement that is given is but of small force, the which rules we will more plainely here declare by the examination which we will make upon every figure which we will here put by example.

A Figure of the yeare 1538. for pope Paule.

[Here follows a ruled chart of the figure set out in pricked star-points: a row of eight dot-figures (the foure mothers and foure daughters), the foure nephewes beneath, then the two witnesses, and the judge at the foot.]

This present fygure here placed, is to shewe that when the fyrst house passeth into the any of the other, yet she passeth not thence although that in some of the other there be fygures like unto that (as before we have sayd) signifying good or ill to come to the querant, according to the importance and accordant signification of the house whereunto she is gone. And so for that in this fygure, the fygure which is in the fyrst, goeth straight into the ninth, by that is signifyed that Pope Paule shall make a long voyage, and because the second goeth into the fift, it signifyeth thereby joy and encrease of substance to his sonnes, and be the passage which is from the sixt unto the eleventh, it sheweth that the servantes of the saide Lorde shall get much friends. And like wise shall ye judge of other fygures by this experience: so that as often and when so ever one fygure doth passe from the first house into the second, or from the second into the third, or from the third into the fourth, or the fourth into the fift, or the fyft into the sixt, you shall judge according to the house where it stayeth and goeth no further. If the fygure and house where it stayeth be good, you shall judge good, and if it be ill ye shall judge ill : and because that in this fyrst example here is come the fygure of Pope Paule, it is not from our purpose to judge some thing more then we have sayde yet before. I say therefore that this yeare the saide Pope Paul, shall make a long voyage, and being come there he shall be angry, and in his returne shall fall sicke of a cather or rume, which is a common disease of this yeare: it shall be in the moneth of October that hee shall be sicke, God preserve and keepe him from death : the end of the yeare shall be to him ill and daungerous : his enemies will be glad of the enterprise of this voyage, and laugh him to scorne. In all this yeare it will be rather judged that he shall die, then live. Beholde what I can judge of his fygure. Now will I put downe an other example to shewe the signification of the fygures when they doe passe into many places, which thing when it happeneth into one fygure, search aide from the house whence any fygure is past, as by way of example. When the first doth goe into the third, search aide of brothers and kinsfolkes : and if it passe into the fourth, search aide of the father ; if it passe into the fift, search aide of the sonne : if it passe into the sixt, search aide of his servants. And thus of all the others according to the signifycation of the house, whether the figure is gone as ye may see by example.

A Figure for my Lord of Ferte.

[Here follows a ruled chart of the figure in pricked star-points: eight dot-figures in the upper row, the foure nephewes beneath, the two witnesses below them, and the judge at the foot.]

My Lord of Ferte being in love with my Ladie Bye, praied mee on a time to make him a fygure to knowe whether hee should obtain his desire, unto whose request I consenting, made unto him this present figure here before : and because the fyrst house doth naturally signify the querant, and the seventh, the thing demaunded which is the woman, were both good fygures and signifyed love, and because the fyrst went into good houses, that is to say, the fourth and fift, which be houses of joy, and into the eight, which is the companie of the woman, and to the two witnesses, and the seventh in the ninth and eleventh, by all this I judged that my saide Lorde of Ferte should have the said Ladie of Bye, and take her to his wife, and that she loved him well, and because the house of the querant went into the companie of the house of the thing demaunded, I judged that this love should augment & daily encrease more and more. And thus by these two examples is manifestly shewed unto you, the translation of the fygures, fyrst by the fyrst fygure, to know when the first goeth onely into one house and there stayeth, and by that meanes what is her signifycation. Secondly by the second example, when the first fygure chaungeth many houses, whereby it is signifyed that they shalbe much the stronger by the counsaile and aide of others, for if she went not out of her first place, without remouing from one to an other, that doth signifie that it doth remaine firme in his opinion and sentence, and needeth not the ayde & succour of the others her companions, and especially when she goeth not into good houses or Angles. And so a fygure is more stronger in the Angle then in any of the other houses, and when a fygure is in a good and an happie house, it signifyeth much good to the querant, and contrariwise when she is in an ill house, it signifyeth much ill, whereof I have thought good at this present to make this small advertisement, to the end ye may the better judge a fygure formed according to the mutations of the figures here put.

Of the company of the house.

Chap. 7.

WHen you finde a good fygure in a good house, it is double good, because the house is good and the fygure also, and signifyeth that without any doubt the querant shal obtaine his demaunde. By the like reason if yee finde an ill fygure in an ill house, it is very ill for the querant, but if ye finde a good fygure in an ill house, it signifyeth good to the querant, but it will not continue, but it taketh away some part of the malice of the house : in like case if yee finde an ill fygure in a good house, it taketh away the malice of the figure, for she would doe harme, but shee can not, keeping alwaies that the good come not to the querant : and for, as much as in this Chapter I have promised to speake of the company of fygures, I will that you doe understand that this companie is of three manners, whereof the one is simple, the other demy simple, and the third compound.

{margin: two dot-figures side by side} The companie simple is of two like figures, as by example, if that yee finde *Aquisitio* in the first house, and likewise in the seconde, and so likewise of all other fygures which in two houses next together be found both of one sort, as if *Conjunctio* be found in the third, and likewise in the fourth.

{margin: dot-figures} When in two houses next together, there be found two fygures a like, and that they be good, ye shall say incontinent that they signifye great goodnesse, and if they be ill, they doe signifie much ill : as by way of example, if ye finde in the fift and ninth *Rubeus*, ye shall say that it signifyeth much ill to the querant, for the question demaunded, and to declare unto you more easely, you must knowe that the second house is alwayes companion of the fyrst, the third of the fourth, the fift of the sixt, and so consequently of the others. If therefore they be both of one element, of one Planet, and of one signe, they signifye much good or much ill, according to their goodnesse or malice. If they be good they signifye that the happe and goodnesse of the querant shall be as well good present as in time to come : as much shall yee judge of the contrarie part if they be evill, and [that] because that the first house signifyeth the time present, and the second the time to come, and likewise of other companies.

{margin: dot-figures} The company demie simple is, when two figures be not both of one sort, nature or condition, although they be both of one Element, and of one Planet, so as the one partie do agree, and the other not, as by example, if it happen that the first be *Aquisitio* and the second *Laticia*, although they be both of the Element, of the aire, and of the Planet [Jupiter], yet be they of diverse signifycations, for that the one of them is of [Jupiter] direct, and the exaltation of [Sun], and the other of [Jupiter] retrogade and the exaltation of [Moon] the one of the signes of [Aries], and the other of the signe of [Taurus].

{margin: dot-figure diagram} The company compound is that which is of divers fygures made one contrarie to an other, as if *Aquisitio* be in the first house, and *Amissio* in the second house, of the which two commeth and is engendred the fygure *Via*, which is a figure of the element of the water, signifying a conjunction of [Sun] and [Moon], which is a triple and compound companie, evill and of great discord, by reason that *Aquisitio* is a fygure of the element of the aire, and of the Planet [Jupiter] in the signe of [Aries] *Amissio* a fygure of the element of the Fire, and of the Planet [Venus] in the signe of [Scorpio]. Which maketh and engendreth the difference of them, and the diversitie and discorde which they have together, out of the which two, as I have saide before, is engendred this fygure *Via*, which is a figure of the element of the water, and of the Planet [Moon] in the signe of [Leo], and is thus contrarie to both the others. Now see how the companie is ill, and that is the cause that when it commeth it can not be judged. And thus of all the others according to the importance of their signifycation, be it good or be it evill.

{margin: dot-figure diagram} There is moreover an other companie of figures which be taken by the pointes on hie of the saide figures, as by example if that *Aquisitio* be in the first house, and *Albus* in the second, the which because they be both good fygures, and be equall of points in the upper part, and that out of them is taken an other which is *Caput draconis* likewise equall in the upper part, it is thereby signifyed that both they be of great force in things good and hoat, and that by the occasion that the fire is the first next unto the Planets, and principall element of all the other, unto whom the first points of the figures be attributed. And for that cause I have set in the fyrst booke the Chapters as well of the Fire, as of the other Elements, to the ende you may knowe their vertues and properties. As much and for the same reason, I have made a Chapter, in the which I have shewed the forme and manner to set the figures by lines, attributing the first to the Fire, as to the fyrst and superiour and principall Element of all the other, the second to the Ayre, the third to the Water, the fourth to the Earth.

The Lord of Garembert of Permeran being desirous of a Ladie to be his friend, desired me on a time to make him a figure to know whether he should have his purpose pretended, unto the which to doe him pleasure I consented, and made him this figure. In the which, because that *Aquisitio* is in the first house, and hath two points on the head, and that his companion hath but one, & by that cause doe not very well agree together : but yet because they be both good figures in case of love, I judged that he should obtaine his purpose, but not without great paine and travaile, because the companie agreeth not very well. But for that the seventh is a good fygure, and attributed unto [Jupiter] as the first is, I sayde that it should be a signe that the woman should love him well, and by that meanes should in the ende marry with him in spight of her children and kindred. Which thing afterward came even so to passe, so that I ryding post with my Lorde of Thays, going to Rome, was advertised thereof and founde my fygure true, and that the Gentleman had marryed the sayde Ladie: which fygure shall serve you for an example to knowe howe to judge the Companie of figures.

Of the company of coniunction. Chap. 8.

THere is an other manner of company in this Science which is called a Conjunction, which is when the first figure passeth in the company or house of the thing demaunded, or to tell you the more truelier, when the figure of the thing demaunded is in company of the first. Which thing I will give you easly to understand by the figure that followeth, and the declaration which I will hereafter make.

A figure to knowe whether the musitian of my Lorde of Lymoges should returne againe into the service of his said Lord and Maister.

[Here follows a ruled chart of the figure in pricked star-points: eight dot-figures in the upper row, the foure nephewes beneath, the two witnesses, and the judge at the foot. Catchword: "Tha".]

THe Lord of Lymoges being gone to Scotland, as well to accompany the Quéene, as to serve the Embassadour of Fraunce, a servant of his a musitian being in an anger departed from his service, desired me to make him a fygure, to knowe whether he shoulde returne againe at any time into the service of the saide Lorde: and thereupon because we had béene of familiaritie together, I did not refuse to make that which he desired me, and taking my penne in my hand, I made him this fygure: and because that the first house thereof is {margin: Fortuna maior} which is a fygure of great goodnes, and Ayre, and [that] the went into the fift, which is Coniunction and Company of the thing demaunded, which is the fift, under the which he compriseth the demaundes and questions which may be propounded touching servants, I judged that the said servant should returne unto his said Maister: and because the first goeth into the tenth, which is the house of Lordes, and in coniunction of the fyrst, the which is found in the ninth, which made me further to judge that his Maister would doe him more good then he expected: because also that the first is found so many times in the saide Figure, it séemed that he shoulde be welcome, and with [good] will entertained into the service of his saide Maister, and that he would do him good, and because that the Figure of the servant is the Fygure of the Element of the water, I judged that the saide servant should passe over the sea, before that he should returne unto his said Maister. And thus following this example, it shall be easie for you to judge a Figure by the company of Coniunction when it hapneth.

Of the occupation. Chap. 9.

THe occupation is when the Fygure of the querant goeth straight into the house of the thing demaunded as hereafter ye shall sée by example, on such wise, that if the first Figure which is the house of the querant be good, and that it passe into the house of the thing demaunded, that is the best signe that may be. But if the figure be ill, it is an ill signe, as by example, if they be two figures of the Fire, and that the Question be made to know which of the two which have sutes or other quarels shall come to an end, it signifyeth that the accord shall bee surely, but it shall not be without fraude or deceipt. If they were both good figures of the Ayre, or of the Water, or of the Earth, they should signifie great goodnes as by the deduction of this example you may see.

THe lord of Clermont of Lodeues on a time did require me to make him a fygure to knowe whether he should obtaine his fathers inheritance, which divers with-helde from him by force, and (as he saide) against reason and equitie, whome hee then had in sute for that cause.
