# Book II, Chap. 7 — Of the seventh house, and of the demaunds therein contayned. Chap. 7.
- leaves: 130-137
- pages: 102-109

[source-chapter: Chap. 7. | Of the seventh house, and of the demaunds therein contayned.]

1 The seventh house, which is the Angle of the Occident, contayneth properly and naturally the significations of the questions and demaundes which may be moved on the contrary of the demaund of any person.

2 Also uppon all debates, sutes in lawe, theeverie and fugitives.

3 The demaundes also which may be made commonly uppon a friend, and uppon any accord of marriage, to know whether it shall take effect or not.

4 The ill will likewise which is betweene two persons, and which way he goeth that taketh his journey.

5 Which of them which playeth at lots, cardes or dice, shall winne or lose.

6 Of two battels ready to fight, which shall have victorie, and on which part it shall be.

7 If the mayde be a virgine or not, and whether shee have any lover, and if she have, then howe standeth the amitie betweene them.

8 This house also contayneth the demaundes which may be made touching marriages, whether there shall happen any strife or debates amongest the doers thereof.

9 Whether a woman or friende lost, shall be recovered againe.

10 If the woman ye would marry be rich, and whether she shall have a good marriage.

11 If the companion appointed to you be a good man or a badde.

12 If there shall be warre or peace, and whether the man shall goe forth, and if the warre shall last long.

13 If the man be of a good esprit and understanding.

14 If the friendshippe betweene two persons be good or ill, trustie or untrustie.

15 Whether the thing stolen be in the house, and whether it shall be found, and who did the robberie be of that house, or be a stranger, and of what sort or clothing he is, and where he is.

16 If the agreement made betweene two persons shall continue.

17 As touching the members of man, this house containeth the demaundes which may be made uppon the buttockes and arse.

These be the principall demaundes and questions which be propounded in this house, the significations whereof she contayneth in such order, as hereafter ye shall finde.

## Acquisitio — House 7
- leaves: 131-132
- dot-pattern: 2,1,2,1

Finding in the seventh house this figure Aquisitio, it signifyeth to make accorde and amitie betweene enemies, the sute in lawe shall be on the plaintives part, the thing is not stolen, but is only scattered away: in the house suspected there are not many theeves, the fugitive will returne againe, the person is of none ill will, it is good to marry and to make marriages, for therein shall be no debate: the wife hath to doe with others then with her husbande, the mayde is no virgine, the woman lost will be found againe, the companion will be good to his wife, there will be no warres but all peace and quietnesse, the person is of a good esprit and entendement, the husbande is not in the house, the man shall not goe to warre, the agreement made betweene two parties shall continue long. For to knowe into what part the person is gone ye must looke of what qualitie, & unto which of the foure parties of the worlde the figure belongeth and is attributed: if it be Orientall, the person is in the East, if it be Meridionall, he is in the South, if it be Septentrionall, he is in the North, if it be Occidentall, he is towarde the West, and thus shall ye judge of all the other fygures.

## Amissio — House 7
- leaves: 132
- dot-pattern: 1,2,1,2

Finding this fygure Amissio in the seventh house, it signifyeth that the open enemie is ill, but he is of no great power, and each thing that he doth, he doth it with an anger and hastinesse, but his anger is soone past, the partie shall lose his sute, the partie suspected is the theefe and hath stolen the thing: the fugitive will not be taken: the woman married will be a whoore: the man is of an ill will, he shall lose at dice and cardes, the marriage will quickly take force, but to no great profite to the one part or unto the other: for warre it is ill, the wife loveth not her husband well, but hath company of others then hee: the mayden is no virgine: the woman lost will not be founde: the man shall goe to warre, but not profite much thereby: there shall be no great feate of armes doone, but onely assaultes and skyrmishes, the accord shall not long last, for because the person is not faithfull: the partie hath no witte but to doe harme, the theefe is not of the house but is runne away, and the thing lost shall not be had againe: there will be none accorde: in all the demaundes which ye may make in this house this figure is ill, but for bawderie.

## Fortuna Major — House 7
- leaves: 132
- dot-pattern: 2,2,1,1

When in this house yee finde Fortuna maior, it signifyeth that the enemie is strong, mightie and of good condition, the plaintive shall winne his sute, there is no theefe in the house, it is good to contract marriage, and for a friend also, the fugitive will returne home againe, the woman is honest and loveth none but her husband, the partie hath no ill minde, the gamester shall winne, there shall be peace and no warre, a woman or friende lost will be recovered againe, the woman is rich, and a great marriage, the companion is good, the person hath good forecast and understanding, the amitie shall endure long. And to be short, this figure is good for each demaund in this house.

## Fortuna Minor — House 7
- leaves: 132-133
- dot-pattern: 1,1,2,2

If in this house ye finde this fygure Fortuna minor, it signifyeth that the enemie is wicked and of an evill heart and affection, strong & mightie, and entendeth much harme: the plaintive shall winne his sute, but not without great paine, travaile and diligent soliciting: the theefe is subtill and craftie: the fugitive will not be found, neither returne againe: the woman is choloricke: the marriage will not be for the small profite that commeth thereof: the man is ill minded: the gamester shall lose or have but small winning: the warres will be great: the woman hath to doe with more then her husband: the mayde is no virgine: the woman or friend lost will not returne againe: the woman is not of the richest: the man shall goe to warre & have the victorie, he hath good knowledge in warres: the theefe will not be found, neyther the thing lost: the accord made betweene two parties will not long continue. In all demaundes which ye may make in this house, this figure is ill, except it be for warre or actes venereall.

## Laetitia — House 7
- leaves: 133
- dot-pattern: 1,2,2,2

Finding this figure Leticia in the seventh house, it signifyeth but small force in the enemie, and besides that he hath none ill minde: the plaintive shall obtaine his sute: the man is not robbed: the fugitive will not returne home againe: it is good for a wife, marriage, and for a friend. If ye make a figure to knowe what your friend doth, and then finde this figure in this place, it signifieth that he weepeth for the great affection he beareth to his friend which is now absent: the person hath no ill will or minde: the gamester shall not winne much: the marriage is sufficient good, so that the tenth consent thereto: there will be no warre but peace: the mayde is a virgine: the wife or paramour loveth none but her husband or friend, and them they love heartely: the woman is not rich: the companion will use him selfe well and faithfully: the thing stolen will be recovered, and he that keepeth it doth it but in jest and pastime: the agreement newly made will not long last. To be briefe, in all things which ye may demaunde in this house, this figure signifyeth a mediocritie.

## Tristitia — House 7
- leaves: 133-134
- dot-pattern: 2,2,2,1

When ye finde this figure Tristitia in this house, it doth signifie the enemy to be strong and mightie, and is ill minded, and will be avenged over all his enemies: the sute is in hazard to be lost on the plaintives part: the theefe or fugitive will not be found: the wife and leaman be good and use themselves loyall: the marriage begunne shall be ended: the partie hath a very evill affection: the gamster shall win, but it shall be by cogging: the mayde is no virgine: the wife loveth her husband, and the leaman her friend: the man shall goe to warre and have the victorie, and when he hath taken his enemie, will let him goe againe: the person hath good understanding and is of great enterprise: the copartner shall doe his part well: the theefe is in the house, and the thing is therein hidden. If that ye make a figure to knowe if that two persons do love together, this figure signifyeth that the friendshippe is but fayned: the accorde made shall last long, so that the tenth consent thereunto, in all other thinges this figure is ill, but it is good to keepe a thing secret which ye would not have to be revealed.

## Puella — House 7
- leaves: 134
- dot-pattern: 1,2,1,1

If by chance ye finde this figure Puella in this house, the enemie hath no minde to worke displeasure unto the other: the sute shall have good successe, the person is not robbed: the fugitive will come home againe, it is good for a woman, a leaman, marriage, and play: there will be no warres, but firme peace: the married woman and the leaman haunte others then becommeth them. If that a figure be made to know whether the friend doe love faithfully, this figure signifyeth that the love is fervent: the woman or leman gone away will be found in the end: the person is of a good esprite and hath understanding in musicke and to play on instrumentes, the thing lost will be found againe, the agreement shall be made and last long: in all the demaundes contayned in this house this figure is good but for warre: by this figure it signifieth that the woman that is married is with childe by others then by her husband.

## Puer — House 7
- leaves: 134-135
- dot-pattern: 1,1,2,1

Finding in this house this figure Puer, it signifieth that the enemy is strong and mightie, and mindeth to kill his enemie, the sute will be lost by the negligence and lacke of paines taking of the solicitors, the theefe is crafty and malitious, the fugitive will not come home againe: it is not good for the woman married for she accompanieth an other man besides her husband: it is ill for marriage, for they will never live without brawling and discord, the man is ill minded: about the game will come strife and quarrell, the mayde is no virgine, the wife is not loyall to her husband, the man is wise and inventious, and especially about warres, we shall have warre, the man that goeth to warre shall have the victorie: the amitie betweene two persons cannot holde: the man suspected hath stolen the thing and is fled: the thing lost shall not be had againe: there shall be none agreement betweene the parties, but mallice shall encrease more and more betweene them. In all questions of this house this figure is ill, but for warre.

## Rubeus — House 7
- leaves: 135
- dot-pattern: 2,1,2,2

If in this house ye finde this figure Rubeus, the enemie is very angry, but he is but of small power, and mindeth to kill his enemie, or else to burne his substance with fire if hee may come by it hansomely, the plaintive shall lose his sute, there were many theeves at that robbery, the fugitive will never returne: it is ill for marriage, for the husband will runne away from the wife and forsake her, by occasion whereof the woman will deale with other men, there will be much debate and strife in the gameing, it is ill for warre, for there will be losse and no profite, and yet the beginning good, and the end ill: the mayde is no virgine, and hath but small substance, the friend loveth not his friend, the companion is not good but doth evill entreate his wife, the man shall goe to warre, but it shall be to his losse, the partie is dull espirited, and hath but small understanding or experience, but in things of the fire: the friendshippe is faigned, it will be none agreement. To be short, in all thinges that may be demaunded in this house, this figure is ill, but for thinges of warre and fire.

## Albus — House 7
- leaves: 135-136
- dot-pattern: 2,2,1,2

When this figure Albus is in this house, the enemie is of no ill minde, neither seeketh to displease the other, or for his death: the partie shall have a good end of his sute, there is no theefe in the house, the fugitive will returne, the marriage shall be with honour, and both parties pleased, the woman shall be good and honest, and the man shall well entreat her, the friende is of good heart, the person hath no ill affection, the woman or friend lost will be found againe, the mayde is a virgine, the woman is rich, there will be no warre, the man is of good esprite and industry, the agreement shall be made. In all things that ye may demaunde in his house this figure is good, but for warres: in case of love, the querant is in love with a rich woman and of great parentage.

## Conjunctio — House 7
- leaves: 136
- dot-pattern: 2,1,1,2

Finding this figure Coniunctio in this house, the enemy is feeble in his reines, and seeketh daily to come to an agreement, the querant shall obtaine his sute to his profite, so that the tenth do consent: it is good for marriage, and better then al the other, for it alwayes doth signify the accomplishment thereof: the theefe that is suspected hath imbezeled the thing lost, and he shall be taken, and in danger to be hanged, the fugitive will not returne, the woman is honest. If the fygure be made to know whether a man shal lie with his lemman, this fygure is good, and signifieth, that he shal enjoy his request, the gamster shall winne, the wife setteth little by her husband, or the lemman by her friend, the maide is no virgine, the man is of great understanding, the companion is good and loyall for his parte, the theefe is of the house, and the thing lost will scarcely be found, because it is out of the house, the unitie shall be made, and long last: for al the things of this house this fygure is meetly, but for war, for the which it is ill.

## Caput Draconis — House 7
- leaves: 136
- dot-pattern: 2,1,1,1

Finding this figure called Caput draconis in the seaventh house, although the enemy be strong, yet will he come to reason without any more to doe: the querant shall winne his sute, there be no theeves, the fugitive will come againe: it is good for marriage, for the woman is good and gentle, it is good for a friend, but the man shall not have his company: it is good for the gamster, the person is of no great ill will, the maide is a virgine, the woman loveth hir husband very well, the woman is rich, the man is of great understanding, the fellowe is good and gentle to his wife, and useth her wel: the thing lost will be found againe: in all the demaundes which ye may demaunde in this house this figure is ill, but for warre, whereunto it is ill, for it signifieth peace. If that ye make a fygure to knowe if that a woman be with childe, and fynde this fygure in this seaventh house, say that it shall be a boy.

## Cauda Draconis — House 7
- leaves: 136-137
- dot-pattern: 1,1,1,2

When ye finde this Figure named Cauda draconis in this house, the enemy is wicked, and seeketh to kill the other by treason or villanie: the querant shall lose his sute, the theefe hath stolen much, the fugitive will never come againe, it is ill for marriage, for the husband will forsake his wife immediately as he is married, ye may thinke the like by a friend: and to be short, ye can not demaund the thing in this house but this fygure is ill for it, but for warres, and to worke treason, and put fire into mines.

## Carcer — House 7
- leaves: 137
- dot-pattern: 1,2,2,1

Finding this fygure Carcer in the seaventh house, the enemy is strong and boisterous, and is secret, and dissembleth in his doings, so that ye can know nothing of him: the querant shall have good successe in his sute: the theefe hath stolen much secretly, the fugitive will never come againe: it is ill for marriage, for there will be some lightnes in the woman, the woman is poore, the woman loveth not her husband: the gamster shall lose in the beginning, but winne in the ende: the man shall be taken in battell and have no victory: the fellow is ill and loveth not his wife, the maide is a virgine: in all the demaundes which ye can demaunde in this house this fygure is ill, except it be to take a prisoner.

## Populus — House 7
- leaves: 137
- dot-pattern: 2,2,2,2

When in this house ye fynd Populus, there is a great number of enemies assembled about mutterings, quarrelles and debates, it is ill for him that sueth by lawe, and also for the theefe, for he hath stolen, the fugitive will not returne: it is indifferent in marriage, but judge as ye fynde in the first, it is good for friendship and company, the maide is no virgine. In all other things which ye may demaunde in this house this fygure is meane but for warre, to the which it is marvelous good, and doth signifie victorie, so that the tenth doe agree thereunto.

## Via — House 7
- leaves: 137
- dot-pattern: 1,1,1,1

If this fygure Via be in this house, the enemy is feeble and of small power, the plaintive shall lose his sute: the theefe is craftie, the fugitive will come no more, for marriage it is ill, for the man will put his wife away, and be separated from her: the gamster shall not winne much, the maide is no virgine. In al the demaunds which ye can put in this house this fygure is ill (except it be for voyages) especially by water.

# Book II, Chap. 8 — Of the eight house and of his demaundes. Chap. 8.
- leaves: 138-143
- pages: 110-115

[source-chapter: Chap. 8. | Of the eight house and of his demaundes.]

1 The eight house called the succedant of the Angle of the Occident, otherwise called the house of death, containeth naturally the questions and demaundes which may be made and propounded uppon the sicknes or death of any man, if he shall live long or die quickely.

2 Also to knowe whether he shall die within a day, month, or yeare.

3 If a person shall die a good death, or else be slaine.

4 Whether shall die first, the father or brother of the querant.

5 If a man be slaine who killed him.

6 Who shall inherite.

7 This house containeth the demaundes which may be made touching the goods and substance of a friend, and uppon the treatie of the marriage of a woman.

8 Who is the secrete Counsailour of your enemy, or of your wife, and whether shee doe keepe faithfull companie or not.

9 Also the demaund which may be made uppon fright or feare of harme to come, as by fyre, burning, or shedding of bloud.

10 Also how the person doth that is from home.

11 The gaine or profite to be doone in a straunge Countrey.

12 When he that is from hence shall returne, in moneth, day, or yeare, and what profite he hath doone whilest he was from home.

13 If the feare ye be in shall come to good or bad.

14 Whether the person unto whome ye have given any thing to keepe will restore it againe or not.

15 Whether he that hath given his money to usurie shall gaine thereby.

16 After what sorte shall the good or harme come to you that you shall have.

17 Finally, this house doth containe the questions which may be made uppon the experience of Nigromancie, invocations of Spirites, Inchauntementes, and other divellish Artes.

18 As touching the partes of mans body, it containeth the questions which may be made concerning the privy and secrete members of man and woman, and therefore when ye fynde any of the fygures following, ye shall judge according as yee shall hereafter fynde.

## Acquisitio — House 8
- leaves: 139
- dot-pattern: 2,1,2,1

When in this eight house ye fynde this Figure Aquisitio, it sheweth the death of the patient before it be long, the man shall be the heire of the dead man, he shall die a faire death in his bed, the brother shall die before the father: the woman is verie rich: that man hath not killed him whome the question is uppon, the woman hath good and honest company: he which counsaileth the enemy is a grave and wise man, so that if he give counsaile to the wife you neede not to feare any harme or inconvenience: the things shall be neither scortched or burned, there shall no bloud be spilt, he that is from home is in health, and shall returne with much silver: it is ill for all things of Nigromancie and divellish Artes: in all things this Figure is good, but for the sicke person, for he shall die.

## Amissio — House 8
- leaves: 139-140
- dot-pattern: 1,2,1,2

Finding this Figure Amissio, it signifyeth the death of the sicke person, if the first and tenth agree unto this: the sickenesse shall not be very long, the man shall loose the succession and have no amendes for the trespas: the man is in the daunger to die on the wheele or some other evill death: the father shall die before the brother, if the fourth and tenth consent: the woman or lemman is not very rich, the partie that is suspected hath slaine a man about a womans cause, the woman hath ill company, he which counsaileth thy enemy and thy wife, is an ill man, and giveth no good counsaile, the feare is nothing: for the experiences of Nigromancie it is ill (except it be for love, and for that it is not very good, for that love will not continue) the man in the strange Countrey shall lose all, and shall bring nothing home, and for to say the trueth, this Figure is evill in all things.

## Fortuna Major — House 8
- leaves: 140
- dot-pattern: 2,2,1,1

When ye find Fortuna maior in this house, the sicke person shall not die at this time, so that the fourth agree with this house, but the sickenes shall be something long, if the tenth doe give consent: the man shall inherite, the man shall die no evill death, the father shall survive the brother, the woman is rich enough, and so is the lemman: the counsailer of the enemy and of the wife is a good man and a secrete, the man needeth not to be afraide: this Figure is ill for the experience of Nigromancie and Arte divellish: in all things which ye may demaund in this house this Figure is good.

## Fortuna Minor — House 8
- leaves: 140
- dot-pattern: 1,1,2,2

When in this house ye fynde Fortuna minor, sometime it signifyeth the death of some great lord, and yet he shall amend, according as the first, sixt, or tenth houses doe consent: the sickenes shall not long endure, the man shall have much adoe about his inheritance, the party suspected for the death of the man hath slaine him indeede: the woman is meetely rich, it is an ill man which counsaileth the enemy and thy wife, the man needeth not to feare any thing at all: it is ill for Nigromancie and diabolicall Artes: in all things which you may demand in this house this Figure is meetely.

## Laetitia — House 8
- leaves: 140
- dot-pattern: 1,2,2,2

Finding this Figure Leticia in this house the patient shall not die of this disease, the man shall be some mans heire, the woman is meetely rich. And to be briefe, this Figure is good in all the demaundes which ye may make (saving for Nigromancie and Arte diabolicall) for the which it is not good, but for to flee in the aire, or to goe on one side or other.

## Tristitia — House 8
- leaves: 140
- dot-pattern: 2,2,2,1

When that Tristitia is in this house, the man is still in a great fright, the man shall inherite by the death of an other. In all other things whereof question may be made in this house, this Figure is evill, but for Nigromancie, for the which it is good: the man shall not be slaine, but shall die some faire death.

## Puella — House 8
- leaves: 140-141
- dot-pattern: 1,2,1,1

When ye fynde Puella in this house, the sicke person is in danger to die, the enemy is in great fright, the woman and leman be rich enough, the sickenes shal not be long, the man shal inherite, the partie hath slaine no man, the father shall live long: in all things which ye may demaunde in this house this Figure is good, except in Nigromancie.

## Puer — House 8
- leaves: 141
- dot-pattern: 1,1,2,1

This Figure Puer in this house, the man shall be slaine, it is ill for the sicke person, for it signifieth death if the others consent thereunto: the sickenes shall not be long, the counsailer of the enemy and of the wife is ill, and counsaileth them to doe ill, the woman is not very rich: in all the demaundes which ye propound in this house this figure is ill, saving for love, and for Nigromancie, whereunto it is good.

## Rubeus — House 8
- leaves: 141
- dot-pattern: 2,1,2,2

Finding this Figure Rubeus in this eight house, the man is or shall be slaine wyth a sworde or a staffe, the sicke person is in danger to die if the other houses doe consent: the enemy and the wife have ill counsaile, the sicknes will be shorte, the man is in great feare, the woman is not rich: touching Nigromancie and the Arte Diabolicall, it is neither good neither sure, because the man is too hasty in his doings.

## Albus — House 8
- leaves: 141
- dot-pattern: 2,2,1,2

When this Figure Albus is in this house, the man shall die in the water, the sicke person shall not heale or amend, so that the others consent thereunto: the disease commeth of a rheume or waterishnes, the woman is rich, he that counsaileth the enemy and the wife is honest, and counsaileth nothing but good, the man needeth not to be in feare, the person hath slaine no man, the father shall live long: in all the demaundes of this house this fygure is good, except for Nigromancie and Diabolicall Arte, in the which it is ill, unlesse it be to make men sing and dance.

## Conjunctio — House 8
- leaves: 141
- dot-pattern: 2,1,1,2

If in this house ye finde Coniunctio the sicke person shal die of this disease, the man in prison shall die by the lawe, the man shall not inherite but lose his sute, the man is in great feare, the counsailer of the enemy and of the woman is ill, the father shall quickely die, the man from home shall not bring home much silver: in all the demaundes of this house this Figure is ill, except for Nigromancie and invocations of evill spirites, for the which it is good.

## Caput Draconis — House 8
- leaves: 141-142
- dot-pattern: 2,1,1,1

This Figure Caput draconis in this house, the person sicke shall be long sicke, but he shall have no other disease, the prisoner shall be quite delivered, but not without great expences & cost: the man shall have his inheritance and overcome the partie: the man is without feare, the counsellour is an honest man, the father shall live long, he that is from home shall profit in his voyage: for all the demaundes which may be propounded in this house this figure is good.

## Cauda Draconis — House 8
- leaves: 142
- dot-pattern: 1,1,1,2

Finding in this house Cauda draconis the person shall be long sicke and in daunger of death, the prisoner is in daunger of his life, the man shall not have the patrimonie that he looketh for, the man is in a marvelous feare: he that is suspected for the murther hath committed the fact, the counsell given to the wife is neither good nor honest, the man shal make no great gaine in the voyage: in all demaunds this figure bringeth no great good lucke, but rather to the contrarie, but for Nigromancie it is good.

## Carcer — House 8
- leaves: 142
- dot-pattern: 1,2,2,1

This figure Carcer in this house, the patient shall be more like to die then to live, the prisoner shall die in prison: it is for succession of heretages good, but not without great paine and travaile, it also is a token of fright and feare. The murther was committed by one which laie in waite to doe it, and did it in some out or darke place, and he that is accused did it, he that counselleth the wife is not of the clearest in the worlde: he that is in trafficke shall doe his businesse well enough, but he is in danger to be robbed by the way: in all the questions which may be propounded in this house this Figure is evill (except it be for Nigromancie) for the which it is marvellous good.

## Populus — House 8
- leaves: 142
- dot-pattern: 2,2,2,2

Finding this figure Populus in this house the sickenes will be long, and the patient in danger of death, but yet in long time he shall amend (so that the first and tenth doe consent) the man shall die in his bed by a rheume, or else be drowned in water: the man is much afraid of men of armes, he that is accused of the murther hath done the fact, and used to be much in his company: the counsellour of the wife and of the enimie is good, the man from home shall not bring much substance home with him: in all things this figure is meetely good (saving for Nigromancie) for the which it is very ill, unlesse it be to make a river to come into a place.

## Via — House 8
- leaves: 143
- dot-pattern: 1,1,1,1

This figure Via in this house, the sicke person shall die on this disease, if the first and tenth consent thereunto: the man shal not inherite: the counsellour of the wife and the ennimy is indifferent, the man in trafficke shall bring nothing home, the wife and the lemman be very poore, the man hath slaine no body, and if he be in prison he shall come forth to his praise: in all the questions which ye may propound in this house this figure is evill, but for Nigromancie, to cause one to be carried away a farre off.
