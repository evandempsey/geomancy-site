**Material following Chapter 12 before Book III (leaves 166–167):**

All sixteen figures are present in both chapters (none missing). The marginal woodcuts print each geomantic point as a small six-pointed star. After the Via entry on leaf 166 come two pieces:

1. **"An advertisement of the contents of the houses of the second booke."** (roman-type heading, leaf 166, printed page 148; paragraph in blackletter, ending at top of leaf 167, printed page 149):

And thus ye have the Figures with their significations by the twelve houses, the which if ye doe well and diligently beholde and examine : you may also judge well all the questions and demaundes of this Arte of Geomancie. Whereunto, that ye may the easier attaine and come unto, I have strained my selfe as much as I can, and more ampler then any doctour, be he Hebrewe, Chaldean, Gréeke, Arabian, Latine, or Egyptian, to write out the most true, certaine, and approoved significations. I will declare them unto you hereafter in the thirde Booke, the which if ye doe understand, you shall likewise understand the first and the second.

2. **"The Prologue of the third Booke."** (italic heading, leaf 167, printed page 149 — misnumbered for 139; text in roman type, signature V3, catchword "The"):

I am affrayde to be accused of great presumption and rashnesse, for that I have joyned Geomancie with Astrologie (a thing which fewe men before me have doone or enterprised, for the difficultie and high understanding which is in the sayde Astrologie) were it not for the assurance which I repose of the good esprites unto whome this my Booke may come, who (as I suppose) shall not finde this conjunction of these two Sciences, strange unto him which will give his studie thereunto, as well for the vicinitie and allyance which they have together (as I have declared in my first Booke) as also for the pastime and recreation which is taken by this Arte, to exercise themselves in things high and wittie. I have therefore according to my small understanding, so well bounde and conjoyned the sayd two Sciences in each of these three books, that he which doth not well understande the first, shall not easely attaine unto the understanding of the seconde and of the third, neither of the third which understandeth not the second, neither of the second which understandeth not the third. Wherefore I desire the Reader of this worke to take the paines to understande the two first Bookes for the easier opening of the third Booke which shall treate simply of the manner to judge the figures with many of the questions alreadie judged.
