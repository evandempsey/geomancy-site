The declaration of the thing which in this Science is called the place of the figures.

Chap. 10.

YOu must understand that most commonly that which in this Arte we call the place, house, or fygure is not but all one thing, so that many times one of these is put in steade of an other, yet there is some difference in the tearming of the one and of the other, because that some places be stronger then other some, for there be foure called angles, and foure succedants, and foure cadants, as we have shewed unto you in a fygure of Astrologie in the end of the first Booke: the angles be the first, fourth, seaventh and tenth, which be the strongest places or houses of al the other, saving that when they move out of their places, they lose parte of their force, and seeke ayde of others. The other foure called house Succedants be meane, the other foure Cadants be weake and feeble. The foure Angles signifye the things which be or shall be done presently, and be done with great industrie, paine and travell. The Succedants signify things which be to come by conjecture: the Cadants signify things past, or those which shall come very late. Moreover, the Angles signify things manifest and publike. The Cadants, things privie and secret. The Succedants, the thinges partely manifest and partely secret. The places finally signify the time, as the Angles time present, the Succedants to come, the Cadants past, as we will hereafter shew by example.

A figure for a Gentleman of the Courte, which serveth for an example to knowe the place of Figures.

A Gentleman of the French Kings Courte being absent a certaine time, and minding to returne and to doe his dutie unto the king, desired me on a time to make him a fygure to know whether it were good for him to goe presently, or else to stay a time. To which demaund, I willing to doe him pleasure, I did consent, and made unto him this fygure, and because the foure Angles thereof which are the first, fourth, seaventh and tenth be good fygures for the querant, and amongest the others the tenth which is the house of the Lord, I judged that it were good for him to goe to the king: and because the Succedants for the most parte be good fygures, I judged that he shoulde shortly have goodnes and profite by the saide Lord, and because the Cadants be all ill fygures, I advertized him not to staie long in the Court, but returne home againe with such speede as he might have no displeasure and harme. You have by this example the manner how to judge a fygure by the Angles, Succedants and Cadants for the time present, past or to come, although that treating of the houses (in the second Booke) I have declared and spoken sufficiently.

Of the Figure of Figures.

Chap. 11.

THis fygure of fygures commeth in a fygure formed, when that one fygure doth agree with an other, that is to say, when the figure agreeth with the place of the thing demaunded: whereof we have spoken here a little before, as of one propounding such a question, as if one shall take a voyage upon him or not. If then ye finde *Aquisitio* in the second house after that he hath beene in the first house: wee say then that those two Figures for one demaunde doe agree together, and that is it which we doe call fygure of fygure. But to tell you more plainely, if it chaunce that a man have made a figure, and the first be found masculine, then say wee that the Fygure dooth agree with the place, and that is called figure of Figure. The like may you judge of a fygure made for a woman, in the which, if the first be found feminine, we doe say that it is figure of figure. And if the question be for way, and that in the third house come that fygure which is called *via*, that is also fygure of fygure, and so consequently of all the others as hereafter by example we will shew you more at large.

And for as much also as the fift is a figure of joy called *Laetitia*, and is in the house of eating and drinking, which is the house of the thing demaunded, that is fygure of fygure likewise: for this cause I judged that the person spake with a woman clothed in white, tall of stature, and a small head, and spake of mirth, & of things of the Church. This is an example when one fygure is found in the house and place of the thing demaunded which is called Figure of Figure.

Of the Aspectes in generall.

Chap. 12.

THe Aspects in the fygure of Geomancie be commonly fiue in number: the first is called *Coniunctio*, the second a Sextile, the third a Trine, the fourth a Quadrate, the fyft an Opposition: wherefore you must know that all the fygures which beholde the fyrst of these aspects: if they be good they be the better, if they be ill they be the worse, and doe weaken according to the houses where they be, and according to the figures good or bad.

Of the aspect of Conjunction.

Chap. 13.

WHen a fygure doubleth and maketh company with an other, then is the regarde of the fygure whereof wee have spoken before, and given example in the Chapiter where wee have spoken of Conjunctions and Companies. But for that there is an other whereof I have not yet spoken, I will nowe in passing over speake a worde or twaine thereof as briefely as I can. You must understand that there be certaine Fygures which doe signifie a conjunction of the Planets: as *via*, which when she chaunceth in any fygure formed, it signifyeth a conjunction of ☉ and ☽, and this conjunction is ill, because they bee two contraries, for ☉ is of nature hote, and ☽ is of nature cold, and therefore this conjunction of *via* is evill when these two Planets be both together for their contrarietie, except the question be made for the way, for then it is good. At this time I will speake no more of this Conjunction, but will now speake of the Sextile aspect.

Of the Sextile aspect.

Chap. 14.

IN the question or figure made the Sextile aspect is, when the first fygure beholdeth the third, and the third the first, and so consequently from three houses to three: and also, when the first beholdes that the 11. is a respect which signifies means and unperfit amitie. Wherefore when in the regarde Sextile ye do finde a good fygure which agreeth with the first, and be of the same Element and of one planet, or that they be both alike, you shall have good fortune and good issue of the question demanded: but contrariwise if ye finde an ill fygure in the said respect which beholdeth the first, and be of the contrarie Planet and Element, as well unto her as unto the other aspects, you shall judge ill of the question made, and of enmitie: whereof I will give you this example of a Gentleman which woulde buie an horse.

A Gentleman minding on a time to buy an horse, prayde me to make him a fygure to knowe whether the horse should be good or not, and also whether it were good to buy him, wherupon I made this fygure, in the which, for because the first is a fygure of ♀ direct, and of the element of the Water, and that the third, which is his Sextill regard, is a fygure of ☉, and of the Element of the Fire, the which two Planets & Elements be contraries, and love not together, I judged that some of his friendes would advise him to buy the saide horse, and because the eleventh is a fygure of the Element of the Ayre, and the first fygure of the Element of the Water, which be two Elements contraries, or at the least doe not agree very well together: I judged that the horse should be scarsely bought, yet because the first is a fygure of ♀, and the eleventh a fygure of ♂ which bee Planets which doe love well together, I say that there should be talke of the buying of the sayd horse, and one of his friends should be the cause thereof. Thus may you judge a fygure by the Sextill Regard, and because it is not enough to have thus judged by the Sextill Regard which hath no respect unto the other rules: I will now judge of the whole rules to shewe unto you an example, also to proceede to the judgement of all the fygures or questions formed. Whereof for as much as the first fygure which is the first Angle, is a good fygure, and the seventh which is the third Angle, and to be short because the foure angles be for the most part good fygures, and do agree all together: thereby is signified that the horse should be bought, although it be to the discommoditie of him which doth buy him, by reason of his goodnesse, and friendship which he beareth to the buyer: which also the seventh and eleventh fygures doe signifye, signifying by their goodnesse that the horse shall be good, and because the sixt which is the house of servants, agreeth with the right witnesse, it signifyeth that a servant shal love the maister which shall buy the horse, and because the two witnesses be good, and that the judge doth agree in Element with the first, I judged that the horse should be bought and sold by the said Gentleman, and because that the part of fortune (whereof wee will speake hereafter) goeth into the eigth house, which is a meane fygure: I say that the servant would so use the matter that his maister would buy the horse. And for that the way of point doth go into the fift, which is a fygure of the Element of the Fire, it is therby signifyed that the Gentleman will quickly buy the said horse.

This fygure judgeth entirely that the horse shall be bought, although the rest of the fygures doe not agree with the Sextill aspect, although that the Sextill aspect be a great part of the judgement of the Figure.

Of the Tryne aspect. Chapt. 15.

THe Tryne aspect is in the fygure, when the first, fift, and ninth fygures doe beholde one an other. And this aspect is naturally good and perfect amitie. The reason why the first, fift and ninth fygures be said to behold one an other by a Tryne aspect: the reason is because that fygures from 5. to 5. doe beholde themselves by that aspect: the which aspect as I have saide before, is of good and perfect amitie, by reason of the house, place and fygure, as by example: if that *Aquisitio* which is a good fygure, be in the first, fyft and ninth houses: or *Fortuna maior*, or *Laeticia* in the saide fift or ninth houses, *Aquisitio* being in the first, which is a very good signe for the querant, for they be all of the Element of the Ayre. As often therefore as you finde a fygure in the fyrst house, and that it goe thence into the fyft or ninth houses, you shall say the Tryne aspect is good and perfect, and signifyeth good happe to the querant, and good issue of his demaund, and if the fygures be all of one Element, it is the better. If yee finde a fygure entring and good in the fyft or ninth houses which passeth from one house to an other, although the fyrst be not like unto it, yet shall the signifycation be of good fortune to the querant, for the practise of which things, I will give you the example following.

The Cardinall Trivulsie, being come Legate into France to entreate of peace betweene the King and the Emperour, I was desired by a Gentleman my friend, to make a fygure to know whether the peace should be or not, and I made unto him this fygure here above: and in the same because that the first fygure is *Coniunctio*, which goeth into the third house, it signifyeth thereby that they should treate of this peace, but because the first is beheld with an ill Aspect fro[m] the fift which is a fygure of ♄ retrograde, called *Tristitia*, it signifyed that there should bee no peace made at this time. But for as much as the ninth is a good fygure, which is the house of Churchmen, it is a signe that the sayde Cardinall would doe what he were able to conclude this peace, but because the seventh is an ill fygure, I judged thereby that the Emperour should have no peace. Also for that the regarde Sextile, of the eleventh fygure is ill. And because the fift, which is the Tryne Aspect, is a fygure of ♄, and that the witnesses and Judge be all of the same Planet. I judged thereby that they should make no peace, and because the second is gone into the eight, which is the house of the substance of the Emperour, I saide that the king should loose much of his substance. And whilest I made the fygure, the king and the Cardinall were at Lyons: and shortly after the Emperour tooke Provaunce untill Ayre, where he stayed not long, but departed, and lost all that he had taken: and so my fygure was true. And this is an example to judge a fygure by the Tryne Aspect, which is a very necessarie rule to judge a fygure well.

Of the quadrats Aspect. Chap. 16.

THe Quadrature Aspect is from the first to the fourth or from the first to the tenth, or from foure fygures unto foure figures. If the first be in the fourth or tenth, it is good for the querant: and as we have spoken of the other rules and aspectes, so will we speake of this, that is to say, that if in the place of this aspect there be a good fygure, or that it be of the same qualitie, and of one Planet, it is a good signe that the querant shall have a good issue and end of his demand. If in the first and twelfth there be *Aquisitio* the quadrature is good: if *Laetitia* be in any of those quadratures, then it shall be meane, because *Laetitia* is retrograde although she be of the same Element and Planet that *Aquisitio* is. And if yee finde in the saide aspect a good fygure so that it be not of the same Element and Planet, as the first is, or the fygure where that quadrature doth beginne: this aspect shall be meane, because the fygure is good. If ye find a figure of one Planet, which is friend to the other, and that she behold him, ye shall judge as well as if *Aquisitio* were in the first house, and *Puella* in the fourth or tenth, as ye shall plainly see by the example following.

WHilest the time that the Emperour Charles the fift was at Nece making his provision to make warre in Provance against the French king, Francis the first of that name: I was requested by the Counte of Touerra (which was desirous to know which day the Emperour should depart from Nece) to make him a fygure. To whom (for that I would not fayle) I made this fygure here before. And for that *Aquisitio* was in the first house, which is a fygure fixed, and of the same Planet that the quadrature which is the tenth is, which is also a signe fixed: for that cause I judged that the Emperour shall not part thence in sixe dayes, and so it chaunced, for hee stayed eight dayes at S. Laurences: and because the seventh is a fygure of ♄ retrograde, and his companion which is the eight, is a fygure of ♂ retrograde: I judged that his long stay in that place should be much to his hinderance, and that should be the cause of his losse and destruction: and because the first is a fygure of ♄ which is a Planet slow fixed, miserable and avericious: I say that the abode which hee there made, was for none other cause, but least he and his Campe should want victuals, because the fyft signifyeth the countrey or land whereunto a person would goe: which thing was true, for his staying there was for none other cause, but to stay for Andreas Dore, which brought him bysket for his souldiers. And because the ninth which is a Trine aspect, and the eleventh which is a Sextill, be ill fygures, I also judged that hee stayed there for money from his friends, which he had borrowed, and it was much for the present necessitie which he sawe: and because the witnesses and the judge be fixed fygures, I judged thereby that he should stay there fiue or sixe daies: and because the first which is the fygure of the Emperour went into the 14. which is the house of sadnesse, I judged that in the end of his voyage (for so much as he could not attaine unto his purpose) he would be sad and angry with himselfe, and so it came to passe, for hee made a short returne with dishonour, losse, and discomfyture of his men.

Of the Aspect of opposition. Chap. 17.

THe regard of opposition in the fygure formed, is, when the fyrst doth beholde the 7. and this is an ill Aspect as well for the distance of the place as for the contrarietie: because that the fyrst is of the Orient, and the seventh of the Occident. And the like it is from the 4. to the tenth, because the fourth signifyeth midnight, and the tenth high noone. If yee finde a good fygure in the first house, and an other in the seventh & that they be both of one Element, qualitie, nature and Planet, then shall yee judge a good issue of ye demaund to the querant, for that amongst them there is no contrarietie or opposition: but contrariwise singuler allyance and friendshippe, as by example, if yee fynde *Fortuna maior* in the fyrst house, and finde him againe in the seventh, or *Puella* in the fourth and the tenth, or contrariwise, if in the fyrst yee finde a good fygure, and an evill in the seventh, you shall say that betweene them is great discord, contrarietie, and enmitie, and thereby you shall judge that it signifyeth ill to the querant, as if in the fyrst house yee finde *Fortuna maior*, and *Cauda draconis* in the seventh, or *Puella* in the fourth, and *Tristitia* in the tenth.

I Being on a time at Fountenbleaue was desired by a friend of mine to make a fygure to knowe if the Cou[n]t of Novelaire were dead or not, which fygure I made as ye see here before: and because the first is a fygure of ☿, and the seventh a figure of ♀, and both agree in one Element, which is of the Water, I judged that when the man which brought the newes came from the Campe, that the saide Countie was not dead, and because that the fourth and tenth be contraries and of great enmitie the one to the other, I judged that he should die of this disease: but because the fygures of the Trine aspect which be the fift and ninth, and those likewise of the Sextill be good, I judged that without any doubt he should live a space after that hee was hurt. But because I sawe other fygures which declared unto me of his death, and that they did agree with the tenth which is *Carcer*, which alwaies in this place signifyeth death, as *Aquisitio* in the eight, & *Coniunctio* for the Judge, I judged that he should die. Which thing I found true, for he dyed beeing hurt on the belly, as you my Lord of Thays doe knowe, and are witnesse of the judgement which I gave: which was found as true as that judgement which I made of the fygure which ye commaunded me to make at Lyons of Marke Antony Cuzanne, finding by my fygure that he was hurt on the necke and on the head, whereof hee dyed: all which happened afterwardes as I found it by my fygure.

The generall rule of regardes and aspectes is such, that the aspect of conjunction is first by two fygures or three agreeing in Element and Planet, or of one Planet onely, and of one Element onely, so that the fygure be good. For if it be good, it is a good signe, and a good conjunction: as if *Puella* which is a good fygure, be in the first house, second, and third, or in the seconde, third, fourth and fift, and so of others, as I have tolde you before in the Chapter of Companie, and of the Conjunction by three fygures which some call a Triplicitie.

The aspect Sextill is from the fyrst to the third, or from the first to the eleventh, or from three to three as is before declared unto you more ampler.

The quadrate Aspect is from foure to foure, the Trine from fiue to fiue; the Opposition from seven to seven. Wherefore when yee fynde a good fygure in any of these Aspectes, you shall judge well and goodnesse: and if yee fynde an ill Planet there yee shall judge ill.
